# North Star Metric — Cadence

**TL;DR**
- North Star: **weekly active teams posting check-ins on ≥3 days that week** — currently 1,420 of ~4,000 paying teams. 🟡 at risk.
- Today we report MRR and total signups; both lag value and hide the week-2 retention bleed that is our #1 priority.
- The tree below cascades into four inputs (acquisition, activation, week-2 survival, depth) — each owned by one person, each with a guardrail.

## Inputs we worked from

- **Core value exchange:** a team member writes an async check-in (yesterday / today / blockers) and a manager reads the AI digest built from them. Value lands when the *team*, not just one person, keeps doing this without a meeting.
- **Activity snapshot (trailing 28 days, as-of 2026-06-14):**
  - 4,012 paying teams; 38,900 active members.
  - 1,420 teams posted check-ins on ≥3 days in their most recent full week.
  - New activated teams last 4 weeks: 96, 88, 104, 91 (avg ~95/week).
  - Week-1 → week-2 team survival: **0.61** (39% of teams that check in during week 1 do not return a qualifying week in week 2 — the bleed we are chasing).
  - Avg check-in days per active member per week: 2.7.
  - Manager digest open rate: 71% of teams with ≥3 check-in days.
- **Business model:** per-seat subscription, $4/user/mo (Team) + enterprise custom. Usage leads revenue with a ~30-60 day lag (teams that stop checking in churn at next renewal).
- **Current metrics:** MRR (owned by the CEO) and total signups (owned by Growth). Neither reflects whether teams keep using the product.

## North Star Metric
**Weekly active teams posting check-ins on ≥3 days that week**

- **Current value:** 1,420 teams · **Baseline as-of:** 2026-06-14 · **Target:** 2,100 teams **by** 2026-09-30
- **Why this captures value:** A team hitting ≥3 check-in days has replaced its standup with the async habit — that is the promise they bought. It counts the *team*, not seats, so a single power-user can't inflate it.
- **How it leads revenue:** more ≥3-day teams → higher week-2+ survival → fewer renewal churns and more seats added → MRR, on a ~30-60 day lag. Revenue is the consequence; this number moves first.
- **Measurement:** a "check-in" = a member submits a check-in. A team is counted in a given week if check-ins were posted on ≥3 distinct calendar days (Mon-Sun) by any members. Week is the team's local week. Trailing measure refreshed daily.

### Three gates (must pass all)
| Gate | Pass? | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Reflects customer value | 🟢 Y | ≥3 check-in days = the standup-replacement habit actually happening, not a login. |
| Leads revenue | 🟢 Y | Teams below the ≥3-day mark in week 2 churn at next renewal; the metric moves 30-60 days ahead of MRR. |
| Measurable & movable | 🟢 Y | Computed from check-in event logs we already store; movable via activation and week-2 nudges. |

## Input metric tree
> NSM shape: **Count/Funnel**
> Relationship to NSM: **Acquisition × Activation × Retention (survival), with depth as the within-team driver — flow equation governs the count, factors are rates/survival, not co-equal multipliers.**

| Input metric | Definition | Owner (team) | Lever (how we move it) | Current → Target | Counter-metric (guardrail) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Breadth / Acquisition** — newly activated teams per week | Teams reaching their first ≥3-day check-in week | Growth (PM + designer) | Onboarding flow that seeds the first 3 check-ins in week 1 | 95/wk → 130/wk | Activated-team trial-to-paid rate (don't buy junk activations) |
| **Activation rate** — % of new teams that hit a ≥3-day week within 14 days | Qualifying-week conversion of signups | Designer + 1 engineer | Day-0 setup wizard, default reminder schedule, Slack install nudge | 47% → 60% | New-member opt-out / mute rate (don't force-spam to activate) |
| **Retention (week-2 survival)** — fraction of week-N ≥3-day teams still ≥3-day in week N+1 | Cross-week team survival / cohort-decay rate | PM + 1 engineer (Blocker Detector) | Week-2 re-engagement nudge; ship Blocker Detector so the digest earns its place | 0.61 → 0.75 | Reminder fatigue: notifications dismissed per member |
| **Depth** — avg check-in days per active member / week | Within-team engagement intensity | 1 engineer + shared analyst | Smart reminder timing, mobile quick-reply | 2.7 → 3.2 | Support tickets per active team (depth shouldn't come from nagging) |

**Threshold validation:** The **≥3 check-in days/week** cutoff is **data-validated**. Activation-threshold analysis on the last 12 cohorts: teams hitting ≥3 days in week 1 retained into week 2 at **0.61**, vs **0.34** for 1-2 days and **0.78** for ≥4 days. Retention lift inflects sharply between 2 and 3 days (+27pts) and flattens after 3 (+17pts to reach 4), so 3 is the "aha" floor that maximizes lift per unit of effort asked of teams. Next-best candidate (≥4 days) gains little survival but cuts the qualifying population ~35%, so it would understate the value habit. ≥3 it is.

**Reconstruction check (Count/Funnel — flow equation):**
- Steady-state qualifying count ≈ new activated teams ÷ (1 − weekly survival) for the active pool, i.e. survival carries last week's teams forward plus new activations:
  `active_this_week ≈ (survival × active_last_week) + newly_activated`
- With current numbers: `0.61 × 1,420 + 95 ≈ 866 + 95 = 961` qualifying carried+new per week at the margin; the standing 1,420 reflects the accumulated pool where inflow (≈95/wk activated) balances outflow (≈39%/wk decay) → equilibrium ≈ 95 ÷ (1 − 0.61) ≈ **244 net weekly contribution stabilizing the ~1,420 base**. Moving survival 0.61 → 0.75 raises equilibrium to 95 ÷ 0.25 ≈ a materially larger standing pool, which is why week-2 survival is the highest-leverage input toward the 2,100 target. Retention enters as the survival factor carrying teams across weeks, not as a co-equal multiplier.

## Cadence
- **Inputs:** reviewed **weekly** by the input owners (Growth, designer, the two engineers, shared analyst) — these move fast and are where the work happens.
- **NSM:** reviewed **monthly** by leadership (PM + CEO) — slow-moving outcome.

## Assumptions made
- "Active" = a check-in event in the trailing 28 days; a "qualifying week" = ≥3 distinct check-in days, assumed measured on the team's local Mon-Sun week.
- Survival 0.61 is the blended week-1→week-2 figure across the last 12 cohorts; per-cohort variance not yet broken out.
- Revenue lag of 30-60 days inferred from renewal-cycle churn timing, not yet causally A/B-confirmed.
- Engineer capacity reflects 1 of 3 engineers on PTO 3 days this sprint — input targets assume that constraint for the current sprint only.

## What this NSM explicitly is NOT
- **Not MRR** (fails the lead gate — revenue lags usage by 30-60 days and can be propped up by discounts while teams quietly stop checking in).
- **Not total signups** (fails the value gate — a signup is not a team that kept the habit).
- **Not total check-ins ever** (fails measurability/now gate — a cumulative count only rises and can't tell us if week-2 retention is improving this week).
- **Not individual active members** (fails value gate — one person checking in alone is not the standup-replacement value; the unit is the team).
