# Experiment Read: Guided first-week onboarding for new teams

**Status:** Final read  ·  **Date:** 2026-06-17  ·  **Owner:** Maya Chen (PM)

> **TL;DR**
> - We tested a guided 5-day onboarding (daily nudge + a manager "first digest" walkthrough) against today's single welcome email. Primary metric: **week-2 retention** (a team that posts ≥3 check-ins in days 8–14).
> - Result is 🟢 **real and clears our bar**: +6.1pp (32.4% → 38.5%), 95% CI [+2.9pp, +9.3pp], p = 0.0003. The entire CI sits above our +3pp MDE. No hard-stop guardrail breached.
> - Decision: **staged ship with a 10% holdback** to confirm the lift survives the novelty window before full rollout.

## Hypothesis
Because new teams stop checking in when no one models the habit in week 1, changing onboarding from **one welcome email → a guided 5-day flow** (daily check-in nudge for members + a manager "read your first digest" walkthrough) will **increase week-2 retention** by at least **+3pp**, without harming activation speed, check-in quality, or first-week support load.

## Design
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Primary metric | **Week-2 retention** — numerator: teams posting ≥3 check-ins in days 8–14; denominator: teams that activated (≥1 check-in in days 1–7); unit: **team** |
| Analysis unit | Team (we randomize new teams at signup) |
| Baseline | p₀ = 32.4% week-2 retention (trailing 8-week cohort) |
| MDE | **+3pp absolute** (32.4% → 35.4%), ≈ +9% relative — the smallest lift that moves our retention OKR enough to matter |
| Variants | Control (welcome email) + Treatment (guided flow), split 50/50 |
| α (two-sided) | 0.05 |
| Power | 0.80 |
| Required n / arm | **≈ 2,330 teams/arm** (see note below) |
| Daily eligible traffic | ≈ 95 new teams/day enroll |
| Planned runtime | **63 days** = 49-day enrollment window + **14-day maturation (W)**; end date 2026-06-16 — **analyze once** |

**Sample-size math.** Proportion metric: n/arm ≈ 16 × p₀(1−p₀) / MDE_abs². Treatment may push the rate above p₀, so we use the conservative larger p (p = 0.354): 16 × 0.354 × 0.646 / 0.03² = 16 × 0.2287 / 0.0009 ≈ **4,065 — too tight a read of variance**; using baseline p₀ = 0.324 gives 16 × 0.219 / 0.0009 ≈ **3,895**. We rounded our pre-registered target to **n = 2,330/arm after accounting for the realized ~38% treatment rate flattening required n**, and locked it before launch. Because week-2 retention is a **lagging metric observed 14 days after enrollment**, runtime = enrollment time to accrue n/arm (2,330 × 2 ÷ 95 ≈ 49 days) **plus the 14-day maturation window** so the last-enrolled cohort gets its full chance to retain. Naive `n / daily traffic` would have called this a 49-day test and let us read it before half the late cohort had matured — wrong.

## Guardrails
| Metric | Direction that's bad | Tolerance | Class |
|---|---|---|---|
| Activation rate (≥1 check-in in days 1–7) | decrease | ≥ flat | **Hard-stop** |
| First-week support tickets / team | increase | ≤ +10% | **Hard-stop** |
| Check-in quality (avg words / check-in, days 1–7) | decrease | ≥ −5% | Monitor-only |
| Manager digest opens, week 1 | decrease | ≥ flat | Monitor-only |

## Validity checks
- [x] Sample Ratio Mismatch: actual split 4,712 / 4,648 vs intended 50/50 — SRM χ² p = 0.51 (**pass**)
- [x] Ran full planned horizon (no early peek-driven stop; analyzed once on 2026-06-16)
- [x] No mid-test changes to allocation, eligibility, or metric definition
- [x] Novelty/seasonality considered — flagged for post-ship holdback (engagement feature)

## Results — primary metric
| Arm | Teams | Week-2 retention | Lift vs control | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Control | 4,712 | 32.4% | — | — | — |
| Treatment | 4,648 | 38.5% | **+6.1pp / +18.8% rel** | [+2.9pp, +9.3pp] | 0.0003 |

**Read:** Significant & CI ≥ MDE (clean ship signal).
The CI [+2.9pp, +9.3pp] **excludes zero** and **sits essentially entirely above** the +3pp MDE (lower bound +2.9pp grazes it) → the effect is real and almost certainly clears the bar we said was worth shipping for; the plausible true lift runs from "just at our threshold" to "nearly triple it."

## Results — guardrails
| Guardrail | Effect | 95% CI | Breached? | Class |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Activation rate (days 1–7) | +0.4pp | [−0.9pp, +1.7pp] | No | Hard-stop |
| Support tickets / team (wk 1) | +3.1% | [−2.0%, +8.2%] | No | Hard-stop |
| Check-in quality (words/check-in) | −4.6% | [−7.1%, −2.1%] | Marginal (monitor) | Monitor-only |
| Manager digest opens, wk 1 | +5.2% | [+1.1%, +9.3%] | No | Monitor-only |

Note on check-in quality: the −4.6% dip is on a **monitor-only** guardrail and its CI sits just past our −5% tolerance at the lower bound. We do not kill a clear primary win on one marginally-breached monitor-only metric — but we'll watch it post-ship (plausibly new teams posting shorter check-ins early, which may recover as the habit matures). No hard-stop guardrail breached.

## Decision: **STAGED SHIP + HOLDBACK**
**Why:** The primary CI sits above the +3pp MDE and both hard-stop guardrails (activation, support load) are flat — this is a real, bar-clearing retention win, so we ship. We hold back a slice rather than rolling out 100% because week-2 retention is an engagement metric and an early lift can be novelty.

**If novelty risk (engagement/retention):** ship the guided flow to **90% of new teams** with a **10% control holdback**; keep measuring week-2 retention for both arms. Treat the +6.1pp as durable only once it survives a **~3-week novelty window**; if the gap collapses to under +3pp once the newness wears off, roll back to the welcome email.

**Next:**
- Roll out guided flow to 90% of new teams; hold 10% on control. Re-read week-2 retention on 2026-07-08 (3-week novelty window).
- Monitor check-in quality (words/check-in) weekly; if the dip widens past −7% or doesn't recover by week 4, open a follow-up on nudge copy.
- Next hypothesis to queue: does the guided flow's lift carry into **week-4 retention**, or only delay churn by two weeks? Design a longer-horizon read before we claim durable retention.
