# Retrospective — Sprint 27 (2026-06-01–2026-06-12)

**Facilitator:** PM (me) · **Participants:** 6 — 1 PM, 3 engineers (1 on PTO 3 days), 1 designer, 1 shared data analyst · **Prime directive:** Blameless — we fix systems, not blame people.

Two root causes drive most of the pain — we never re-estimated after the ML scoring scope tripled mid-sprint, and the analyst hand-off has no definition-of-ready. ## 1. What went well (keep doing)
- **Async retro pre-write covered the PTO gap** (×4 mentions) — Devs wrote Well/Wrong/Confusing in the shared doc before this call, so Raj (PTO Wed–Fri) still contributed. Worth protecting: it kept the absent voice in the room.
- **Retention nudge experiment shipped clean** (×3) — The week-2 re-engagement email went live with proper holdout + Amplitude event wiring on the first try. Small, well-scoped, one owner.
- **Pairing on the timezone bug** (×2) — Mara + Jin paired and closed the digest timezone offset in ~2 hours instead of it bouncing across days.

## 2. What went wrong (hurt us)
- **Blocker Detector scope tripled mid-sprint, never re-estimated** (×5) — Started as "flag stale check-ins"; by mid-sprint it had grown to confidence scoring + manager override UI + a backfill job. We kept the original 8-pt estimate. This is the single biggest driver of the spill.
- **PR reviews stalled during PTO** (×3) — With Raj out 3 days and the other two heads-down on the Detector, two PRs sat 2+ days. Reviewer load wasn't redistributed when we planned around the PTO.
- **Two defects escaped to prod** (×2) — A digest duplicate-send (10 teams got two digests) and the timezone offset. Both shipped Friday afternoon with no second reviewer on the release.

## 3. What was confusing (unclear / surprised us)
- **Analyst hand-off had no definition-of-ready** — The shared data analyst is split across teams; we assumed the Detector training data pull was queued, she assumed we'd file a ticket with the schema. Three days lost to a hand-off nobody owned.
- **"Beta to 5 teams" had no owner for the go/no-go call** — When the Detector wasn't ready, it was unclear who decided to hold vs. ship internal-only. The call got made implicitly by the engineer at 5pm Friday, not as a decision.
- **PTO capacity wasn't reflected in the committed number** — We knew Raj was out 3 days at planning but still committed 21 pts. Was that a deliberate stretch or an oversight? Nobody was sure in the room.
