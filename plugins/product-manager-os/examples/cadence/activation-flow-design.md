# Activation Flow Design — Cadence first-run, self-serve teams (Slack app directory)

**TL;DR.** Activation = **a team reaches 2 members posting a check-in in week 0** (the digest only has signal when ≥2 people post). Today **71% of signups activate blended, but self-serve teams lag at ~58% (inferred, see Assumptions)** — and the path is **6 steps / ~12 min**, gated on a teammate invite that happens *before anyone has seen a digest*. The biggest leak is **Step 4 (invite teammates) → premature-value-ask**: we demand the admin recruit their team before they've felt one ounce of value. The redesign **defers the invite until after the admin sees their own first digest**, seeds a pre-filled first check-in, and adds a day-1 Slack trigger — cutting the critical path to **3 steps**. Expected: self-serve activation **~58% → ~72%**.

## The activation event (the aha)
- **Event:** A team where **≥2 members post a check-in within week 0** (the first 7 days). This is Cadence's standing P0 definition and it's the right one: the manager digest is only valuable once it aggregates more than one person — a solo admin posting alone never sees the product's actual job (cross-team visibility) done.
- **Why this is the aha (not a proxy):** The felt reward is the **manager reading a digest that surfaces a real update or blocker they didn't already know** — that only happens at 2+ posters. Cohort data backs the gate: activated teams (2+ posters in wk0) carry a ~38% retention floor; teams that never get a second poster churn invisibly inside week 0 and never appear in the retention table.
- **Time-to-activate target:** **2+ posters within 48h** of workspace creation (tightening the wk-0 definition — the faster the second poster, the higher the wk-1 rhythm).
- **Rejected candidates:** "Connected Slack" (⚙️ setup — required, but no value felt). "Admin posted first check-in" (one poster ≠ digest value; this is the trap the current flow optimizes toward). "Invited 8 teammates" (an *investment* we currently demand pre-reward).

## Current first-run path
| # | Step | User intent | On critical path to value? | Friction | Drop-off |
|---|------|-------------|----------------------------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Install from Slack app directory, create workspace | "Try the standup replacement" | ✅ value-adjacent | low | 6% |
| 2 | Slack OAuth — approve scopes | "Connect my workspace" | ⚙️ setup | **scopes look scary** — Intercom tag `oauth-scopes` = 14% of wk-1 tickets | 11% |
| 3 | Pick check-in time + edit the 3 default questions | "Configure how it works" | ⚙️ setup | config decisions before any value | 7% |
| 4 | **Invite 8 teammates** (required to proceed) | "…I have to recruit my team first?" | ⚙️ setup, **mis-sequenced** | 7/11 managers asked "who *has* to answer?"; solo evaluators stall here | **🔴 24%** |
| 5 | Admin posts their own first check-in into an empty form | "Answer the 3 questions" | ✅ value-adjacent | "answering into the void" (9/9 team interviews) — no read signal | 9% |
| 6 | Wait for ≥1 teammate to post → first digest | "Did this work?" | ✅ **value** | depends entirely on Step 4 invitees showing up | gated |

**Time-to-value today:** ~12 min of setup + a multi-hour wait for a second poster. **Hard dependencies gating the aha:** the teammate invite (Step 4) and at least one invitee actually posting.

## The drop-off, diagnosed
- **Biggest leak:** Step 4 (invite teammates) — **~24%** of self-serve admins quit here, the largest single fall on the path.
- **Type:** 🔴 **premature-value-ask** — we demand the admin's biggest investment (recruiting their whole team) *before* they've seen a single digest. This inverts the Hooked loop: investment is supposed to come *after* the variable reward, not as a gate to reach it.
- **Root cause:** A self-serve admin from the app directory is usually a **solo evaluator** kicking the tires. Forcing them to invite 8 people before they understand the value means they either (a) bounce rather than spam their team for an unproven tool, or (b) invite half-heartedly and never reach a second poster. The blended 71% activation hides this: sales-assisted teams (a human sets up the cadence) sail through, self-serve teams stall at the invite. This is the same self-serve vs. sales-assisted gap the cohort table shows downstream at the week-2 cliff — it starts *here*, in first-run.

## Redesigned path (re-sequenced)
**Moves applied:** **defer the teammate invite until after the admin sees their own first digest** · **pull-forward value** by seeding a pre-filled first check-in from the admin's linked issue tracker · **add a day-1 Slack trigger** to bring a second poster in.

| # | Step | Trigger | Action | Reward (the value felt) | Investment (loads next trigger) |
|---|------|---------|--------|-------------------------|---------------------------------|
| 1 | Install + connect Slack (plain-language scope explainer inline) | Slack app directory click | Approve scopes (now explained: "we read channel membership to route check-ins, nothing else") | "That was painless" — removes the `oauth-scopes` fear | Workspace exists; we know the admin's Slack identity |
| 2 | **Seeded first check-in** — admin lands in a *pre-filled* check-in (yesterday/today/blockers drafted from their linked tracker, editable) instead of an empty form | In-app, immediately post-OAuth | Edit + post one check-in (30 sec, not a blank page) | **Admin sees their own check-in render in a real digest preview** — the artifact, instantly, with one poster | A posted check-in + the admin's question prefs (skips old Step 3 config) |
| 3 | **"Add one teammate to see the real thing"** — invite is now framed as unlocking value already glimpsed, and asks for **1 teammate, not 8** | In-app nudge right after the digest preview | Invite 1 teammate (Slack-native, pre-written message) | The 2nd poster posts → **admin reads the first real cross-person digest = the aha** (variable: depends on what the teammate wrote) | 2 posters in the digest; the team now has a live cadence to defend |

**Deferred to post-activation:** custom check-in time, full question editing, inviting the rest of the team (7 more), advanced digest config — all moved *after* the first real digest, when the admin is hooked and willing to invest.
**Time-to-value, redesigned:** ~2 min to the admin's own seeded check-in + a short wait for the 1 invited teammate (was ~12 min + recruit-8-then-wait). **Steps to aha:** **3** (was 6).

## Empty states & nudges (at the fragile steps)
| Step | Empty state (zero-data screen teaches…) | Nudge: trigger → channel → intent | Exit condition (stops firing when…) |
|------|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Step 2 (first check-in) | **No empty form ever** — the check-in arrives pre-filled from the tracker with a one-line "edit anything, then post"; the digest-preview panel shows exactly where this post will land | If admin opens but doesn't post in 10 min → in-app banner: "Your first check-in is drafted — post it in one click" | Admin posts first check-in |
| Step 3 (1st invite) | Digest preview shows the admin's lone post with a greyed "+ add a teammate to fill this in" slot — the empty slot *is* the teaching | No teammate invited 24h after admin's first post → Slack DM to admin: "Add one teammate to see Cadence do its actual job — here's a ready message" (max 2 sends, 48h apart) | 2nd member posts a check-in (= activated) OR 2 sends reached |
| Step 3b (invited teammate) | Invited teammate also lands in a **seeded** check-in (drafted from *their* tracker), never a blank form — directly attacks "answering into the void" | Teammate accepts but hasn't posted in 24h → Slack DM: "Your draft check-in is ready, post in one tap" | Teammate posts (team is now activated) OR 2 sends reached |

## Instrumentation (to prove it worked)
- **North-star activation metric:** **% of new self-serve teams with ≥2 members posting a check-in within 48h of workspace creation.**
- **Step events to fire:** `workspace_created`, `slack_oauth_completed`, `seeded_checkin_shown`, `first_checkin_posted` (props: `seeded=true/false`, `actor=admin/teammate`), `first_invite_sent` (prop: `invite_count`), `second_poster_posted` → `team_activated`.
- **Read method:** compare **self-serve signup cohorts after the redesign ships vs. the pre-redesign self-serve cohorts** on the 48h-2-poster rate — not a blended before/after (the sales-assisted segment would mask the effect and seasonality would confound it). Ideal: hold back 20% of self-serve signups on the old flow as a control. Re-pull at **4 weeks**, watching self-serve specifically.
- **Guardrail:** **week-2 self-serve retention must not fall** — a faster activation that pulls in lower-intent teams could juice the activation metric while worsening the downstream week-2 cliff (the same cliff `cohort-analysis` flagged at P1→P2 for self-serve). Also watch support volume (don't trade `oauth-scopes` tickets for "where did my config go?" tickets) and posted-check-in quality (seeded check-ins shouldn't be shipped unedited and meaningless).

## Assumptions
- The 2-posters-in-wk0 activation event is **correlated** with retention (the ~38% floor sits on activated teams); it is not yet **causally proven** that forcing earlier activation lifts retention rather than just pulling forward teams that would have activated anyway — the self-serve holdout above is what settles it.
- Self-serve activation of ~58% is **inferred** from the cohort split (self-serve P1 77% vs. sales-assisted 84%, with the gap originating pre-P1); the exact self-serve wk-0 2-poster rate should be pulled directly before committing. The 71% is the established blended signup→P0 rate.
- Most self-serve admins have a linked issue tracker we can seed a draft check-in from. If tracker-linkage is low at signup, the "pre-filled from tracker" reward weakens and we fall back to a templated example check-in.
- Numbers are illustrative, consistent with Cadence's ~$3M ARR / ~4,000-team base; replace with shared-analyst figures before building.

## What I'd test first
**Ship the re-sequence + seeded first check-in (Steps 2–3) and measure the 48h-2-poster rate against a self-serve holdout.** It targets the single biggest leak (the 24% premature-invite drop) with the highest-leverage move (defer the invest-ask, pull value forward), and it's the same activation gap that feeds the week-2 retention cliff and the team-invite growth loop — one fix, three downstream wins. The 1-teammate-instead-of-8 framing and the seeded teammate check-in are the second wave once the admin-side lift is confirmed.
