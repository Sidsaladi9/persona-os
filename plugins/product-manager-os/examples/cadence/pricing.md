# Pricing & Packaging: Cadence

**TL;DR**
- We charge per *active checked-in teammate*, not provisioned seats — that's what's killing week-2 retention renewals, and it ties our invoice to realized value. 🟡
- Four tiers: Solo (free), Team ($5/active user/mo), Business ($8/active user/mo), Enterprise (Contact us). Blocker Detector is the gated feature that makes Team→Business the obvious upgrade.
- We are underpriced. Van Westendorp puts the Team optimal at ~$6, not today's $4. We recommend $5 and grandfathering current teams.

## 1. Value Metric

**Chosen metric:** active checked-in teammate per month (a user who posted ≥1 check-in in the billing period)
**Why:** it grows only when teams actually use Cadence, is trivial to predict (managers know their headcount), hard to game (a fake check-in still surfaces in the digest, so gaming is self-defeating), and cheap to meter (we already log every check-in).

| Candidate metric | Scales w/ value? | Easy to predict? | Hard to game? | Cheap to meter? | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Provisioned seats | N | Y | N | Y | drop — inflates the bill with dormant seats, the exact thing driving week-2 churn |
| Active checked-in users | Y | Y | Y | Y | chosen |
| Number of check-ins | Y | N | N | Y | drop — spiky, punishes daily-standup teams, easy to feel nickel-and-dimed |
| Digests sent | N | Y | Y | Y | drop — tracks manager count, not team value |

## 2. Pricing Model

**Model:** tiered per-seat, billed on **active not provisioned** users
**Rationale:** Cadence's value scales with people collaborating asynchronously — classic per-seat. But our week-2 retention problem is partly a *billing* problem: teams provision 30 seats in a burst of optimism, 11 keep checking in, and at renewal they see "we're paying for 30, using 11" and downgrade or churn. Billing active users lowers headline ARPA (~$4 → effective ~$3.10 on a team that half-adopts) but makes the renewal defend itself, and gives us a built-in expansion mechanic as adoption climbs.
**Price floor:** cost-to-serve ≈ $0.40/active user/mo (compute + AI inference for the digest and Blocker Detector). Never price any tier below $0.40/user — all proposed tiers clear this by 10x+.

## 3. Willingness-to-Pay (Van Westendorp — per segment)

Run the four questions separately per segment (median responses, per active user/mo):

| Segment | Too cheap | Bargain | Getting expensive | Too expensive |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Solo | $0 | $0 | $2 | $4 |
| Team | $2 | $4 | $7 | $11 |
| Enterprise | $6 | $10 | $16 | $24 |

Derived points per segment (canonical intersections):

| Segment | PMC (too cheap × expensive) | PME (bargain × too expensive) | Acceptable range (PMC→PME) | OPP (too cheap × too expensive) | IPP (bargain × expensive) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Solo | $0 | $1 | $0–$1 | $0 | $1 |
| Team | $3 | $5 | $3–$5 | $6 | $5 |
| Enterprise | $8 | $12 | $8–$12 | $13 | $13 |

- **Source:** n=64 Team responses + n=22 Enterprise responses from the May in-app survey; Solo proxied from 9 free-trial interviews + competitor anchors (Geekbot free tier, Standuply ~$3–4/user). Confidence: medium on Team, low-medium on Enterprise, low on Solo.
- **Note:** we never blended these into one curve — Team WTP tops out near $11 "too expensive" while Enterprise doesn't flinch until $24. That spread is exactly what justifies the Business and Enterprise tier prices. Today's flat $4 sits *below* the Team OPP ($6), confirming we're underpriced for the segment we sell to most.

## 4. Good–Better–Best Tiers

We use four named tiers (Solo free + three paid) so the free tier handles low-WTP solo users without dragging the paid anchor down. Good/Better/Best below maps to Team/Business/Enterprise.

| | Solo (Good) | Team (Better) ★ Most popular | Business | Enterprise (Best) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $0 | $5/active user/mo | $8/active user/mo | Contact us |
| **Value metric** | up to 3 active users | up to 50 active users | up to 200 active users | unlimited / custom |
| **For** | solo founders, 1-team trials | eng teams at 20–80 person companies | multi-team orgs, 80–200 people | 200+ or security-led buyers |
| **Key features** | async check-ins, basic digest | check-ins + AI digest + integrations (Slack, GitHub) | everything in Team + **Blocker Detector** + cross-team rollups + analytics | everything + SSO/SAML, audit log, SLA, custom roles, dedicated CSM |
| **Upgrade trigger** | hits 3 users / wants AI digest | wants Blocker Detector / needs cross-team view | needs SSO / 200+ users / security review | — |

**Anchor:** Enterprise ("Contact us") is positioned to make Business read as reasonable, and Business is positioned to make Team feel safe. The Blocker Detector is the single fence between Team and Business — it's our launch bet, so we gate it deliberately rather than spreading it everywhere.
**Target tier:** Team — expect ~55–60% of paying teams here.
**Tier gaps:** Team→Business ~1.6x on the unit rate but the *plan-price* jump for a typical 25-user team is $125→$200/mo (~1.6x); Business→Enterprise negotiated. *(Gaps stated on total plan price, not per-unit rate — per-unit actually holds flat here because the fence is the feature, not volume.)*

## 5. Money-Left-on-the-Table Check
- [x] Top tier uncapped ("Contact us") — biggest accounts aren't capped
- [x] No single tier holds >70% — Team projected ~55–60%, Solo absorbs free users, Business takes the multi-team middle
- [x] Solo (free) captures low-WTP solo users without cannibalizing Team — the 3-active-user cap + no Blocker Detector forces the upgrade once a real team forms
- [x] Enterprise captures high-WTP buyers — Enterprise WTP runs to $24, so SSO/audit/SLA buyers should land here
- [x] Price ≥ floor ($0.40/user) on every paid tier

## 6. Expansion Path

**Outgrow moment:** a Team-tier org crosses 50 active users, or a manager asks for the Blocker Detector / a cross-team rollup after week-3 adoption sticks.
**Mechanic:** active-user billing auto-expands the invoice as adoption grows (no sales touch), and an in-product prompt surfaces "3 blockers went undetected this week — see them on Business" once a team has ≥2 simultaneous active check-in streams.
**Expected expansion:** target net revenue retention 110%+ — the metric makes seat expansion automatic, and Blocker Detector is the deliberate paid pull.

## 7. Open questions & next test
- Biggest assumption: that moving from $4 flat to $5 active-user billing nets *up* despite the lower per-team active count. Model says yes for teams >40% adopted; needs validation.
- Test 1: A/B the Team price at $5 vs $6 on new signups for 4 weeks; watch trial→paid conversion and week-2 retention.
- Test 2: re-run Van Westendorp on Solo at n≥50 — current Solo read is the weakest input and decides whether the free cap should be 3 or 5 users.
- Migration note: grandfather all current teams at $4 for 6 months, then migrate to active-user $5 with a "your bill likely drops at current adoption" message — turn the reprice into a retention moment, not a hike. 🟢
