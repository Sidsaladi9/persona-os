# Blocker Detector — Product Spec

**Author:** Priya Menon · **Status:** Draft · **Last updated:** 2026-06-17
**Reviewers:** Marcus Lee (eng lead), Dani Okафор (design lead), Tomás Reyes (PM peer), Jenna Hartwell (VP Product, sponsor)

## TL;DR
Managers read every check-in but still miss the work that's quietly stuck — by the time a blocker surfaces in a digest, it's often days old. We're adding Blocker Detector: it reads each team's check-ins, flags at-risk work, and surfaces those flags at the top of the daily digest. The one outcome we're driving: managers act on real blockers sooner, measured by flagged items that get a manager response within 24h.

## Problem
Cadence replaces standup with async check-ins plus an AI digest. Managers tell us the digest is good at summarizing *what happened* but bad at surfacing *what's at risk* — a person writing "still wrestling with the auth migration, third day on it" reads the same as routine progress. The signal is in the text; we don't extract it. The cost: blockers age in plain sight, and "we should've caught that sooner" is the most common complaint in churn interviews.

- **Evidence:** In 12 of our last 18 churn interviews (Q1–Q2 2026), the manager cited "didn't catch problems early enough" as a top-2 reason for cancelling. Support tagged 47 tickets "wish the digest flagged X" in the last 90 days. Internal analysis of 2,300 sampled check-ins found ~9% contained an unflagged blocker phrase ("stuck", "blocked by", "still", "waiting on", "not sure how").
- **Why now:** Week-2 retention is our #1 priority this half, and digest value is the lever — teams that get an obviously-useful digest in week 1 are 1.8x more likely to still be checking in at week 2. A sharper digest is the cheapest retention win available before we touch onboarding.

## Goals
[Goals = outcome targets; Success metrics = how we instrument them. Each number stated once here.]

1. Managers respond to real blockers faster — measured by **% of flagged items that get a manager action (reply, react, or status change) within 24h of flagging**, target **≥ 55%** within 6 weeks of GA.
2. Flags are trustworthy enough that managers don't tune them out — measured by **precision (flags marked "useful" or actioned ÷ total flags)**, target **≥ 70%** by end of beta.
3. Digest value contributes to week-2 retention — measured by **week-2 check-in retention for teams with Blocker Detector on**, target **+4 pts vs. the off cohort** by end of Q3.

## Non-goals
- **Permanent:** We do not auto-resolve, reassign, or change the status of any work item. Blocker Detector flags; humans act.
- **Permanent:** No predictive "this project will slip" forecasting. We flag what's stated in check-ins, not model future risk from velocity data.
- **Permanent:** No Slack/Jira/Linear write-back in this feature. We read check-in text only; integrations are a separate track.
- **Deferred (see Phase 3):** No cross-team or org-wide blocker rollups for the manager-of-managers view this release — single team only.
- **Deferred (see Phase 2):** Not customizing detection sensitivity per team this release; one tuned default for everyone first.

## Users & use cases
- **Primary user:** Engineering manager / team lead (the digest reader) — job-to-be-done: spot the one or two things on my team that need me *today* without reading every word of every check-in.
- **Secondary user:** The team member writing the check-in — need: when I flag that I'm stuck, I want it to actually reach my manager, not get buried.
- **Key use cases:**
  1. As a manager, I want at-risk items pulled to the top of my digest so that I act on them before standup-replacement value erodes.
  2. As a manager, I want to dismiss a flag that isn't really a blocker so that the system learns and stops crying wolf.
  3. As a team member, I want my "I'm blocked on X" to be detected and surfaced so that I don't have to also ping my manager separately.
- **Out of scope users:** Execs / VPs wanting a portfolio risk view (Phase 3). Individual contributors browsing other teams' blockers.

## Solution overview
Each time a team's digest is generated, Blocker Detector reads that window's check-ins and classifies each into a blocker / not-blocker decision with a short reason and a severity (at-risk vs. blocked). Flagged items render as a **"Needs attention"** section pinned to the top of the digest, each showing who, the work, the one-line reason, and the source check-in quote. The manager can act inline — reply, react, or dismiss. Dismissals and actions feed back as labeled signal to tune precision over time. If nothing is flagged, the section is hidden so absence reads as "all clear," not "broken."

**Happy path:** Team submits check-ins → digest generation runs detection → at-risk items pinned to top of digest with reason + quote → manager opens digest, replies/dismisses inline → action logged, flag resolved.

## Requirements
| # | Priority | Requirement |
|---|----------|-------------|
| R1 | P0 | The system must classify every check-in in the digest window as blocker / not-blocker before the digest is sent. |
| R2 | P0 | When ≥1 blocker is detected, the digest must render a "Needs attention" section at the top listing each flag with the person, a one-line reason, and the verbatim source quote. |
| R3 | P0 | When 0 blockers are detected, the digest must omit the "Needs attention" section entirely (no empty header). |
| R4 | P0 | The manager must be able to dismiss a flag in one action, and the system must record the dismissal as labeled training signal. |
| R5 | P0 | Detection must not block digest delivery: if classification fails or exceeds the time budget, the digest sends without the section and the failure is logged. |
| R6 | P1 | Each flag must carry a severity of "at-risk" or "blocked," ordered blocked-first within the section. |
| R7 | P1 | The manager must be able to reply to or react to a flag inline, and that action must mark the flag as actioned. |
| R8 | P1 | The system must deduplicate the same blocker recurring across consecutive days into a single aging flag, not a new flag each day. |
| R9 | P2 | Managers can configure detection sensitivity per team (low / default / high). |

## Success metrics
- **North-star for this feature:** % of flagged items with a manager action within 24h (Goal 1 — target ≥ 55%).
- **Supporting metrics:** Flag precision (Goal 2 — ≥ 70%); week-2 retention lift on the on cohort (Goal 3 — +4 pts); median age of a blocker at first manager action.
- **Guardrail metrics:** Digest delivery success rate must stay ≥ 99.5% (R5); digest generation P95 latency must not increase more than 1.5s; flag volume must not exceed ~3 per digest on average (more reads as noise and trains managers to ignore).
- **Measurement plan:** Emit `blocker_flagged` (team_id, check_in_id, severity, reason), `blocker_actioned` (flag_id, action_type, latency_ms), `blocker_dismissed` (flag_id), and `digest_sent` (had_blocker_section bool). Tracked in Amplitude; precision labels join dismissals + actions against total flags.

## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] Given a digest window with at least one check-in containing a stated blocker, when the digest generates, then a "Needs attention" section appears at the top with that item, its reason, and the source quote.
- [ ] Given a digest window with no blockers, when the digest generates, then no "Needs attention" section or header appears.
- [ ] Given a flag in the digest, when the manager dismisses it, then the flag is removed from the active list and a `blocker_dismissed` event is recorded with the flag_id.
- [ ] Given the same blocker phrased similarly on two consecutive days, when the second digest generates, then it appears as one aging flag, not two.
- [ ] Edge case: when the classifier errors or exceeds its time budget, the digest still sends on schedule without the section, and a failure is logged.
- [ ] Edge case: when a check-in is empty or contains no work content, the system classifies it not-blocker and emits no flag.
- [ ] Instrumentation: `blocker_flagged` fires with team_id, check_in_id, severity, and reason on every detected flag; `blocker_actioned` fires with latency_ms on manager response.

## Assumptions
- Stated blockers live in check-in *text* and are detectable from language alone — we do not need ticket/velocity data to catch most of them.
- Managers read the digest near-daily; surfacing at the top of the digest is sufficient distribution (no separate push/email needed for v1).
- A precision floor (≥70%) matters more than recall for trust — managers will forgive a missed blocker far longer than they'll tolerate false alarms.
- ~1–3 flags per digest is the right volume for our median team size (5–8 people); larger teams are rare enough to handle in Phase 2.

## Rollout / phasing
- **Phase 1 (MVP):** Detection + "Needs attention" section + dismiss + core instrumentation (R1–R5), single team scope, one tuned default sensitivity. **Gate:** precision ≥ 70% on the beta cohort before widening.
- **Phase 2 (fast-follow):** Severity ordering, inline reply/react actioning, cross-day dedup, per-team sensitivity (R6–R9).
- **Phase 3 (later):** Cross-team / org rollups for the manager-of-managers view.
- **Launch plan:** Feature flag, beta with 15 hand-picked teams for 3 weeks, then 10% → 50% → 100% gated on the precision and guardrail metrics. **Rollback trigger:** digest delivery success drops below 99.5%, or beta precision stays below 70% after two tuning passes.
