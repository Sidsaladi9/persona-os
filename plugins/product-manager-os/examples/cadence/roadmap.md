# Cadence roadmap — 2026-06-17

**TL;DR:** We're pulling the AI Blocker Detector into Now because week-2 retention won't move on activation tweaks alone — managers churn when the digest feels like noise. To make room (capacity = 3 Now bets), we're shipping a rules-based v1 of the Detector, not the full ML version, and pushing the Slack-thread digest redesign to Next. Nothing else gets added without something leaving.

Strategic themes this period:
1. Fix week-2 retention — get teams past the week-1 cliff to a sustained check-in habit.
2. Make the digest worth a manager's attention — surface signal, not a transcript.
3. Hold the line on async value vs. "just use Slack" — give managers something Slack can't.

Capacity note: small team (3 engineers, 1 on PTO 3 days this sprint; 1 designer; shared data analyst). We can run **3 Now bets** honestly. "Now" is full.

## Now  (committed, in-flight — high confidence)

- **Get teams past the week-2 cliff with a re-engagement nudge loop** 🟡
  - Why now: 41% of teams that check in week 1 stop by week 2. Telemetry shows the drop concentrates in teams where 2+ members miss two consecutive check-ins — the habit dies before it forms. A targeted nudge (Slack + email) to the team lead when participation slips is the cheapest lever we have.
  - Confidence: High (on problem), Med (on the exact nudge that converts)
  - Dependencies: Needs participation-decay signal from the data analyst (shared resource — flagged as the main schedule risk).
  - Owner: Priya (eng) + shared analyst

- **Flag at-risk work from check-ins so the digest earns its open (Blocker Detector v1 — rules-based)** 🟡
  - Why now: Managers tell us the digest reads like a wall of text and they skim it. The Detector is the bet that turns the digest from a log into a decision tool — it pulls out check-ins that signal stuck or slipping work and puts them at the top. Shipping the **rules-based v1** now (keyword + sentiment + "no movement for N days" heuristics), not the ML version, so it fits one Now slot.
  - Confidence: High (rules v1 ships this quarter), Low (ML version — deferred to Later)
  - Dependencies: Reuses the digest pipeline; blocks the digest redesign (we want the redesign to surface Detector output, so it sequences after).
  - Owner: Marco (eng)

- **Cut check-in friction so first-week completion holds** 🟢
  - Why now: Foundation for theme 1 — if the daily check-in is a chore, no nudge will save retention. In-flight: shorter default prompt, one-tap "same as yesterday," and timezone-aware reminder timing.
  - Confidence: High
  - Dependencies: None.
  - Owner: Designer + Priya (eng)

## Next  (sequenced, strong intent — details may shift)

- **Redesign the digest around Detector output so managers see the 3 things that matter first** 🟡
  - Why now: Strong intent, but it depends on the Detector producing reliable flags first — building the UI before the signal is real would mean rework. Sequenced right behind Detector v1.
  - Confidence: Med
  - Dependencies: Blocked by Blocker Detector v1 (needs real flags to design around).
  - Owner: Designer (lead) + Marco

- **Give admins a participation health view so they can act before a team goes dark** 🟡
  - Why now: Extends the retention work from per-team nudges to org-level visibility — a buying reason for the EM persona. Strong intent; deferred only because Now is full.
  - Confidence: Med
  - Dependencies: Builds on the participation-decay signal from the Now nudge work.
  - Owner: TBD (post-PTO capacity)

## Later  (directional bets — deliberately fuzzy)

- **Upgrade the Blocker Detector from rules to ML** — once v1 proves managers trust the flags, train on labeled outcomes to catch blockers the heuristics miss. Bet, not a commitment.
- **Async standup → async planning** — extend check-ins toward lightweight sprint/goal tracking, pushing further from "just use Slack."
- **Integrations beyond Slack** (Teams, Linear/Jira context in check-ins) — directional; depends on where the EM buyer demand concentrates.

## Changes this update

- **Pulled the Blocker Detector into Now AND narrowed it in place — same decision, made together.** The Detector is the highest-leverage bet for theme 2 (digest worth opening), and theme 2 is upstream of week-2 retention: managers who find the digest useful keep their teams checking in. To fit it into one Now slot without blowing capacity, we shipped the **rules-based v1, not the full ML version** — the ML version moves to Later. This is the forced trade-off: we bought the outcome (flagged at-risk work) by shrinking the how (rules, not ML).
- **Pushed the digest redesign from Now → Next** — it can't start until the Detector produces real flags to design around. Building it earlier would mean rebuilding it. Moving it out also freed the designer to pair on check-in friction in Now.
- **Held "Now" at 3 bets despite pressure to add the admin health view** — it's a real buying reason, but Now is full and nothing else was worth dropping for it. It's a strong Next, not a quiet fourth Now slot.
- **Risk flag, not a move:** the participation-decay signal depends on the shared data analyst, and one engineer is on PTO 3 days this sprint. If the analyst signal slips, the re-engagement nudge loop slips with it — we'll move it, not pretend the horizon holds.

## Parking lot

- **Weekly mood/sentiment report for managers** — below the line; no clear tie to retention or digest value, and risks feeling surveillance-y to teams. Needs validation before it earns a slot.
- **Gamified check-in streaks** — tempting for theme 1 but unproven for B2B teams and easy to game; parked pending a retention read on the nudge loop first.
- **Custom check-in templates per team** — real demand, but a configuration feature, not an outcome bet this period. Revisit once the core habit holds.
