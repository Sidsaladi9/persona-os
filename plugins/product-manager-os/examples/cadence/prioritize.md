# prioritize — worked example (Cadence)

> Generated by the Product Manager OS `prioritize` skill. Demo company: Cadence.

---

**TL;DR**
We scored 6 backlog features with RICE against week-2 retention. 🟢 Smart check-in reminders and a 4-day onboarding sequence top the list — both are cheap and hit the exact moment teams drop off (after week 1). 🟡 Blocker Detector ranks 4th on retention alone, but we're pulling it forward as a strategic exception: it's the committed Q3 launch and the differentiator vs. Geekbot.

**Framework:** RICE — *many comparable features competing for the same small-team roadmap, and we have rough per-account reach from product analytics. Default for backlog triage.*
**Goal/metric this ranks against:** Week-2 retention — % of teams still posting check-ins in their second week (today: 41%; target: 60% by end of Q3).

## Scale (locked before scoring)

- **Reach** — # of paying teams the feature touches per quarter. We have ~4,000 paying teams; new-team inflow is ~600/quarter, and ~2,300 teams hit the week-1→week-2 cliff each quarter.
- **Impact** — 3 massive / 2 high / 1 medium / 0.5 low / 0.25 minimal, judged on how directly it moves a team from week 1 to a sustained week-2 habit.
- **Confidence** — 100% solid data / 80% good evidence / 50% educated guess / <50% genuine speculation (a flag to validate before committing, not a forbidden score).
- **Effort** — person-months, **sized by engineering**, not the PM. Sprint has 3 engineers, 1 out 3 days on PTO.
- **Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort.**

## Recommended order + why

1. **Smart check-in reminders** — best return for the effort. One person-month, aimed straight at the week-1→week-2 cliff, and the most common churn reason in support is simply forgetting. Ship first.
2. **4-day guided onboarding sequence** — smaller reach (new teams only) but the highest-impact lever: the week-2 habit is set or lost in the first few days. Ship alongside reminders; they compound.
3. **Digest reply + reactions** — promising but resting on an unvalidated impact assumption. Run a quick read on whether "my check-in goes into the void" predicts churn before committing the 1.5pm.
4. **Custom question templates** — cheap, broad, low impact. A fast filler if there's slack capacity, not a retention bet.
5. **Slack-native check-in** — defer. Big build, speculative impact, and it partly arms the "just use Slack" alternative. Revisit after the cliff is fixed.

## Close calls to discuss

- **Smart reminders (3,680) vs. custom templates (3,200)** — separated by impact (2 vs. 0.5), not effort. Both are cheap and touch many teams. Flip only if reminder data shows nudges annoy teams more than they help (watch opt-out rate in the first cohort).
- **Digest replies (767) vs. Slack-native (300)** — both ride 50% confidence. Digest replies win on much lower effort. If the "no one reads it" churn hypothesis is wrong, digest replies collapse and this gap closes.

## What would change the ranking

- **Reminders' 80% confidence is load-bearing for #1.** If the first cohort shows nudges don't lift week-2 posting (or drive opt-outs), reminders fall behind onboarding. Measure week-2 retention on the reminder cohort before scaling.
- **Digest replies' impact is a guess (50%).** If we confirm "my check-in goes unread" is a top-3 churn driver, this jumps from #3 toward #2 and justifies more effort.
- **Blocker Detector's reach (1,400) assumes teams post enough signal to detect blockers.** If fewer active teams generate usable text, both its reach and retention impact shrink further.

## Strategic exceptions (scored against a different goal)

- **AI Blocker Detector (ranks #4 on retention) — pulled forward to a Q3 commitment.** It scores low against *week-2 retention* and it scored honestly — high effort, speculative near-term retention impact. We're building it this quarter anyway because it serves a different goal: it's our committed Q3 launch and the clearest differentiator against Geekbot and Standuply ("digest that just summarizes" vs. "digest that flags at-risk work"). The override is deliberate. To de-risk it, we'll scope a thin v1 (rules + light model on existing check-in text) so it doesn't starve the two retention bets above it, and we'll instrument whether surfaced blockers correlate with teams staying — which, if true, would re-rank it on retention next pass.
