## Captured house style

This is the content written to `memory/house-style.md`.

### Voice & tone
- Direct, plain, no hype. State the status, then the risk, then the ask.
- First person plural — "we". ("We shipped detection to 12 internal teams.")
- Lead with the honest assessment, including bad news. No softening.
- Short sentences. Cut adjectives. Numbers over qualifiers ("~80% precision", not "good precision").

### Formatting conventions
- **Every doc opens with a 2–3 line TL;DR** carrying the status marker and the single most important ask.
- Headings in **sentence case**.
- Prefer **bullets and tables over prose**. Use prose only when a point needs a sentence of nuance.
- **One page** unless depth is genuinely needed; don't pad.
- **Status markers, used consistently:** 🟢 on track / 🟡 at risk / 🔴 off track.
- Calls to action are explicit and dated ("30 min with Design on the digest UI by Thu").

### Terminology & naming
- Users are **"teams"** — never "customers", "users", or "accounts" in any doc.
- Product surfaces have fixed names: **check-in**, **digest**, **Blocker Detector**. Use these exactly; don't paraphrase ("the standup thing", "the alerts feature").
- Preferred: "async check-in", "manager digest", "at-risk work".
- **Banned in customer-facing docs:** internal codenames. **"Project Lighthouse"** (the Blocker Detector build) must NOT appear in anything a team sees.

### Document templates
**Stakeholder update** (captured from the sample — overrides the default skill structure):
1. **TL;DR** — 2–3 lines: status marker + the headline + the one ask.
2. **What we did** — bullets, with numbers.
3. **Open question / risk** — name the single biggest risk plainly.
4. **The ask** — explicit, owned, dated.

### Branding
- Primary color: **Cadence Indigo `#4F46E5`**.
- Typeface: **Inter**.
- (Low confidence — see assumptions.)

### Audience defaults
- Default reader: **engineering managers / team leads** at 20–200-person tech companies.
- They skim. Front-load the answer; respect their time.
- Internal updates can name the codename; customer-facing ones cannot.
