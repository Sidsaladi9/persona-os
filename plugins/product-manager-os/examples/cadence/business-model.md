# Business Model — Cadence
Framework: Lean Canvas · Date: 2026-06-17 · Owner: Priya Nandakumar (PM)

**TL;DR**
- Cadence is a running business (~$3M ARR, ~4,000 paying teams), so problem/segment are validated by people paying — we pull these from data, not interviews, and default the risk lens to Solution + Key Metrics.
- The model's compounding number is week-2 retention, and it's broken: ~58% of teams that finish week 1 stop checking in by week 2. That single block is both unproven and fatal.
- Riskiest block to validate first: **Key Metrics** (week-2 retention). Cheapest test below — a 2-week habit-nudge experiment on new teams.

## The Canvas

| Block | Contents |
|---|---|
| **1. Problem** | 1. Daily standup meetings burn 20-30 min/person/day and break deep-work focus for distributed engineering teams. · 2. Managers lose visibility into who's blocked until it's too late to unblock. · 3. Existing async tools get set up, then quietly die — teams stop posting after a week.<br>**Existing alternatives:** Zoom/Slack huddle standups · Geekbot, Standuply · a hand-rolled Slack channel + reminder bot · nothing (manager DMs people). |
| **2. Customer Segments** | Distributed/hybrid software teams at 20-200 person tech companies. **Buyer:** engineering managers / team leads. **User:** ICs who post check-ins.<br>**Early adopter:** EMs of 5-12 person remote eng teams across 2+ time zones who already killed the live standup and are limping along with a Slack reminder bot. |
| **3. Unique Value Proposition** | "We help engineering managers see what their team is doing and what's at risk — without a single standup meeting, and without nagging people to post."<br>**High-level concept:** Async standup that reads itself — a digest, not a meeting. |
| **4. Solution** | P1 → 2-min written check-in replaces the live standup; no calendar slot. · P2 → AI digest surfaces blockers and at-risk work to the manager (Blocker Detector). · P3 → Smart, escalating reminders + streaks so the habit survives past week 1. |
| **5. Channels** | Content/SEO on "async standup" + "kill the standup" (proven) · Slack App Directory listing (proven) · founder-led + EM community / Slack-group word-of-mouth (proven) · outbound to Series A/B eng orgs (unproven) · partner co-marketing with PM/eng tools, e.g. Linear (unproven). |
| **6. Revenue Streams** | Subscription, seat-based. Price: $4/user/mo (Team); Enterprise custom (SSO, audit, SLA). · Who pays: the company, via the EM's budget or eng-leadership line item. · ~4,000 paying teams ≈ $3M ARR. |
| **7. Cost Structure** | CAC ~$140/team (mostly content + a light sales touch) · COGS: hosting + LLM inference for digests/Blocker Detector (~$0.90/active team/mo, rising with AI usage) · Fixed: 8-person team (eng, design, PM), data tooling.<br>**Unit-economics check:** ARPA ~$62/team/mo, gross margin ~84%. If a team stays 18 mo, LTV ≈ $940 vs CAC $140 → LTV/CAC ≈ 6.7×. Healthy — **but the whole calc hinges on retention, and many teams churn inside 30 days, which silently collapses LTV.** |
| **8. Key Metrics** | Activation: team completes ≥3 check-ins in week 1 · Paid conversion: trial → paid · **North Star: week-2 team retention — % of teams still checking in (≥3 days) in their second week.** Today ~42% (i.e. ~58% drop off). |
| **9. Unfair Advantage** | 4,000 teams' worth of check-in + blocker-outcome data — a labeled corpus of "what early signals preceded slipped/at-risk work" that no new entrant or generic Slack bot has. This is what makes the Blocker Detector accurate and is genuinely hard to buy. |

## Riskiest Block to Validate First

Risk = Uncertainty × Fatal (1–25). Scored all nine blocks; existing-business mode, so Problem/Segment/Revenue are treated as measured (low uncertainty) and the lens defaults to Solution + Key Metrics.

| Block | Uncertainty (1–5) | Fatal if wrong? (1–5) | Risk (U × F) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Problem | 1 | 5 | 5 |
| Customer Segments | 1 | 4 | 4 |
| UVP | 2 | 4 | 8 |
| Solution | 4 | 4 | 16 |
| Channels | 3 | 3 | 9 |
| Revenue Streams | 1 | 3 | 3 |
| Cost Structure | 2 | 3 | 6 |
| Key Metrics | 5 | 5 | 25 |
| Unfair Advantage | 2 | 4 | 8 |

**Riskiest block: Key Metrics (week-2 retention)** — the entire LTV/CAC and ARR-growth model assumes teams keep checking in, but ~58% go quiet by week 2; if we can't move that number, the business is acquiring teams into a leaky bucket and the unit economics are a mirage.

**Cheapest test:** Run a 2-week A/B on the next ~200 new teams — control gets today's onboarding, treatment gets the habit changes (escalating smart reminders + streaks + a "first blocker caught" aha moment in week 1). No new infra; reuse the reminder service. · **Pass/fail signal:** treatment lifts week-2 team retention from ~42% to ≥55% (≥13pt absolute, stat-sig) within the 2-week window. · **Timebox:** 3 weeks (1 to ship the nudge variant, 2 to run).

## Open assumptions
- [ASSUMPTION: COGS of ~$0.90/active team/mo holds as Blocker Detector usage scales] — track LLM-inference cost per active team weekly once Blocker Detector is GA; revisit pricing if it crosses ~$2.
- [ASSUMPTION: the labeled blocker-outcome corpus actually makes Blocker Detector meaningfully more accurate than a generic LLM prompt] — backtest detector precision/recall against held-out resolved blockers before claiming it as the moat externally.
- [ASSUMPTION: outbound to Series A/B eng orgs can hit CAC ≤$140] — run a 4-week outbound pilot and measure blended CAC before scaling that channel.
