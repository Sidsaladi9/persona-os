# Ideal Customer Profile — Cadence (async standups + manager digest)

**TL;DR:** Our best-fit teams are 8–25-person product/eng squads at 50–200-person tech companies, distributed across 2+ time zones, already living in Slack, with a hands-on EM who owns the standup. They activate fast and retain because async check-ins solve a real timezone/meeting-load pain. We churn hardest among tiny co-located teams that adopted on a whim and never felt the pain — and we should stop chasing them.

Owner: PM (Product) · Date: 2026-06-17 · Based on: 9 retained + 7 churned teams (paid book of ~4,000 teams)
Confidence: **Medium** — bands reverse-engineered from 16 named accounts in the win/loss tracker + a week-2 retention cohort export, not the full book. Unit economics are from the Q1 board deck (blended). Validate against the `retention_cohorts` table (check-in frequency by week) and Stripe MRR-by-account before sales/marketing filters lock.

## One-line ICP

> Distributed product or engineering teams of **8–25 people** at **50–200-employee tech companies**, spread across **2+ time zones**, already running daily standups **in Slack**, with a **hands-on EM/team lead who owns the ritual** and is feeling meeting-load or timezone pain acute enough to change this quarter.

## Why these accounts (the value mechanism)

- **We create the most value when:** a team is distributed enough that a live standup is genuinely painful (someone always joins at 7am or 10pm), and the EM already cares about the ritual — so async check-ins remove real friction instead of adding a new chore.
- **Time-to-value for a good-fit team:** under 10 days — value lands the first week the whole team posts check-ins without a meeting and the EM reads the digest instead of attending.
- **Unit economics (blended, Q1 board deck):** avg ACV ~$960/team/yr (≈20 seats × $4/user/mo) · CAC ~$310 (mostly PLG + light sales-assist) · gross margin ~82% · payback ~4mo · LTV:CAC ~5.5:1 at target retention. Exclusions below are defensible on margin too: sub-5-seat teams carry the same support load at a fifth of the ACV.
- **Evidence:** every retained team in the sample had **week-2 check-in rate ≥ 70%** and **2+ time zones**. Every churned team but one was **single-timezone and/or under 6 active users** — they never hit the pain the product removes.

## Behavioral & situational fit

- **Trigger** — went remote/hybrid or opened a second-timezone office in the last 6–12 months; or a new EM inherited a team and wants to cut meeting load. Signal: job posts for "remote," "distributed," or a newly-hired EM/Head of Eng on LinkedIn.
- **Existing workflow** — already runs a daily standup, today as a live call or a manual Slack thread someone has to chase. Signal: a `#standup` / `#daily` channel; reminder bots already installed.
- **Pain intensity** — meeting fatigue or timezone overlap pain is named, not theoretical — they've complained about standup in a retro this quarter. Signal: inbound mentions "too many meetings," "standup doesn't work async," or trialed Geekbot first.
- **Readiness** — the EM will mandate it and read the digest; team is in Slack daily. Signal: EM-level person creates the workspace (not an IC kicking the tires).
- **Buying committee** — econ buyer: **EM / Head of Engineering** (owns the budget line and the ritual) · champion: same EM or a team lead · users: the 8–25 ICs posting check-ins.

## Anti-signals — who to AVOID (disqualifying)

| Anti-signal                                     | Why it burns us                                                        |
|-------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Single-timezone, co-located team                | Never feels the pain; a live standup is fine; churns by week 2         |
| Team under 6 active members                     | Async ceremony has no payoff at that size; low ACV, same support cost  |
| Not on Slack (Teams-only, or no chat platform)  | Core surface absent; integration gaps; high-touch onboarding, low fit  |
| IC signed up, no EM sponsor                      | No one mandates check-ins; usage decays after week 1                   |
| Wants it as a "project status tracker" / PM tool | Use case we only half-serve; sets false expectations, bad reviews      |
| Asks for heavy custom workflows / on-prem        | Drags roadmap; one-off; not our motion at this price point             |

## Qualifying checklist (rep/marketer can run this live)

- [ ] Team size 8–25 · [ ] Company 50–200 employees · [ ] 2+ time zones
- [ ] Slack is primary comms · [ ] EM/lead owns signup · [ ] Already runs a standup

→ **5+ checked, no anti-signal = ICP.** 3–4 = monitor / nurture. <3 or any anti-signal = disqualify.

## Where to find them

- **Data / filters:** Apollo / LinkedIn Sales Nav — headcount 50–200, dept "Engineering 8–25," title "Engineering Manager / Head of Engineering," keyword "remote" or "distributed."
- **Intent:** G2 "standup tools" + "async communication" category browsers; comparison searches "Geekbot vs," "Standuply alternative"; teams trialing Geekbot.
- **Communities & events:** Rands Leadership Slack, /r/engineeringmanagement, Lead Dev conference, remote-work communities (e.g. We Work Remotely audience).
- **Integrations / marketplaces:** **Slack App Directory** (primary channel — teams find us where they already work), Jira/Linear/GitHub marketplace listings.

## Boundaries

- **Implied addressable teams:** ~tens of thousands of distributed 8–25-person eng/product squads at 50–200-person tech firms globally — narrow enough to target, large enough to scale toward our ARR goals.
- **Deliberately excluded:** tiny co-located teams (no pain), non-Slack shops (no surface), IC-led signups with no EM (no retention), and "project tracker" buyers (wrong job). These are real deals we'd otherwise take — and the ones poisoning week-2 retention.
- **Revisit when:** Blocker Detector ships (may widen fit to larger / less-distributed teams who buy for risk visibility, not async) or after the next retention-cohort refresh — revisit by **2026-Q4**.
