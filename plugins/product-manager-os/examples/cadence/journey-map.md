# Journey Map: Maya (engineering manager) — first-time team: signup → week-4 retention

**TL;DR**
We mapped one team's experience from signup through week 4. The valley is week 2 (🔴 emotion −2), right where 38% of teams stop checking in — managers get a quiet digest and team members feel nagged with no payoff. The single biggest lever is making week-2 value visible to both sides, not adding features.

**Persona:** Maya — engineering manager at a 60-person SaaS company, runs an 8-person team across two time zones, killed daily standup to buy back focus time.
**Scenario / scope:** Adopt async check-ins as the team's standup replacement · signup (Maya creates workspace) → end of week 4 (team has a check-in habit, or has lapsed).
**Evidence base:** 11 manager interviews (Apr–May), 9 team-member interviews, activation funnel (n=412 teams, last 90 days), Intercom support tags (last 60 days), 23 NPS verbatims, 4 CS churn-call notes. Sourced cells outnumber assumptions — this is **not** a hypothesis map, though the week-3/4 emotional reads lean on fewer data points.
**Data gaps / not available:** No session recordings for the digest email (open/click only). Team-member emotions in weeks 3–4 inferred from 9 interviews, not instrumented. No analytics on Slack-reminder dismissals (event not yet tracked).

Lowest point: **The week-2 dip** — the emotional valley (−2) lands exactly on the 38% check-in drop-off and the digest open-rate collapse (61%→34%). Writers feel ignored, managers can't see value, and nothing intervenes. This is the priority-one stage.

## Biggest opportunity per stage
| Stage | Biggest opportunity (one only) | Tied to pain | Evidence strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sign up & invite | Plain-language Slack scope explainer + clear "who's required" toggle in invite flow | "scopes look scary" + "who has to answer?" | strong |
| First check-in | Close the loop for writers — show read receipts / reactions / one-line manager acknowledgement | "answering into the void" | strong |
| Week-1 rhythm | Restructure the digest so blockers surface to the top, scannable in 20 seconds | "flat wall of text, blockers buried" | medium |
| The week-2 dip | Make value visible both ways at the moment of risk: surface "your check-in flagged X" to writers and a "this week you caught N blockers" summary to Maya before the dropoff window | reminders feel like nagging; value invisible | strong |
| Habit or lapse | Lapse-risk nudge that names the loss ("your team has skipped 3 days — here's the visibility you'd lose") | frictionless silent lapse | medium |

## Prioritized shortlist (act on these — top 3–5 only)
1. **Two-way week-2 value surfacing** — week-2 dip · impact: H · evidence: strong · effort: M · owner: Maya (PM) + designer — directly targets the −2 valley and the 38% drop; highest-leverage point on the whole map. Pairs naturally with the Blocker Detector launch (writers see "your check-in flagged X").
2. **Close the loop for writers** (read receipts / reactions) — first check-in · impact: H · evidence: strong · effort: L · owner: Eng (full sprint, not PTO eng) — removes "answering into the void," the root cause cited in 9/9 team interviews; low effort, upstream, so it wins the tie-break and compounds into week 2.
3. **Digest blocker-first redesign** — week-1 rhythm · impact: M · evidence: medium · effort: M · owner: Designer — makes the digest worth opening, slows the open-rate collapse, and is the surface the Blocker Detector ships into.
4. **Lapse-risk nudge that names the loss** — habit/lapse · impact: M · evidence: medium · effort: L · owner: Eng — cheap safety net for teams that slip past week 2; ship after #1–3.

🟡 Sequencing note: one engineer is on PTO 3 days this sprint. Land #2 (low effort, highest evidence) this sprint; #1 spans into next sprint alongside Blocker Detector.
