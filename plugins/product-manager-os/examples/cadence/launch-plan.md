# Launch Plan: Blocker Detector

**TL;DR**
We're launching Blocker Detector — the AI that reads check-ins and flags at-risk work before a manager has to ask. T2 launch (touches the core digest surface, aimed at retention), targeting all Team-plan managers via a staged rollout starting 2026-07-14, full availability 2026-07-21. Success = 35% of active managers open a flagged blocker in week 1; abort if false-positive rate exceeds 30%.

## Launch summary
- What: Blocker Detector reads daily check-ins and surfaces at-risk work in the manager digest, with a one-line reason and a link to the source check-in.
- Why it matters: Managers stop reading digests when they're just a wall of "what I did" text. Blocker Detector gives the digest a reason to open every day — it tells you where to look without a meeting.
- Tier: T2 — notable feature on a core surface, tied to our top retention priority.
- Why this tier (evidence): Hits the boundary rule "touches a core surface OR moves a top company metric" — it ships inside the digest (core surface) and is the primary lever for fixing week-2 retention (top company metric: only 41% of teams still check in by day 8). Not T1 — no new product or repositioning, no press/analyst motion warranted.
- Date: Staged rollout 2026-07-14 (internal + 10 design-partner teams) → 50% on 2026-07-17 → 100% on 2026-07-21.

## Audience & message
- Audience / segment: Engineering managers and team leads on the Team plan ($4/user/mo) who manage an active check-in (≥3 members checked in last 7 days). ~2,600 of our ~4,000 paying teams qualify at launch.
- Core message: Digests turn into noise once your team grows past a handful of people (problem). Blocker Detector now reads every check-in and flags the work that's stuck or slipping, with one line on why and a link to the check-in (what changed). You skim the flags, not the whole digest — and you catch the blocker before it's a missed sprint (why they care).
- Proof: Design-partner data from 11 teams over a 3-week beta — Blocker Detector flagged 142 at-risk items; managers acted on 61% of them; flagged teams kept 78% of members checking in at day 8 vs. 41% baseline. Quote from beta lead Priya Nair (EM, Loftwell, 34-person eng org): "I used to scan 20 check-ins every morning. Now I read three flags and know exactly who to DM."

## Channels
- In-app — new "Blockers" section at the top of the digest + a one-time tooltip walkthrough on first flagged item. Owner: Dana Okafor (designer).
- Email — announcement to all qualifying Team-plan admins/managers (~2,600 teams) on 2026-07-21; staged-rollout heads-up to design partners on 2026-07-14. Owner: Marcus Reyes (you, PM, drafting) / Lifecycle (sending).
- Changelog — public changelog entry + docs page on how flags are generated and how to tune sensitivity. Owner: Marcus Reyes (PM).
- Blog — single post: "Why we built Blocker Detector" with the beta data and Priya's quote. Owner: Marcus Reyes (PM, draft) → reviewed by founder.
- In-product support content — Intercom macro + help-center article for "Why did Blocker Detector flag this?" Owner: Sam Liu (support lead).
- Not doing (and why): No press/analyst, no webinar, no paid social — those are reserved for a T1 motion. This ships inside an existing surface to existing paying teams; amplifying beyond email + blog would over-launch it.

## Success metrics
- Primary: % of active managers who open at least one flagged blocker in week 1 — target 35% by 2026-07-28.
- Secondary: action rate on flags (DM, comment, or reply) ≥ 25%; day-8 check-in retention on Blocker Detector teams ≥ 65% (vs. 41% baseline).
- What failure looks like: false-positive rate (manager marks a flag "not a blocker") exceeds 30% in the first 5 days, OR fewer than 15% of managers open any flag by 2026-07-28. Either signals the flagging is noise, not signal.

## Risks & rollback trigger
- Risk: Flagging is too noisy → managers tune it off or ignore the digest entirely. → Mitigation: launched conservative sensitivity default tuned on beta data; daily FP watch for 5 days; sensitivity control shipped on day one.
- Risk: Flag latency or load on the digest-generation job at 100% volume. → Mitigation: staged rollout caught this at 50%; Jordan monitors job duration during the flip.
- Risk: Support overwhelmed by "why was this flagged" tickets. → Mitigation: help-center article + Intercom macro live before launch; Sam staffed for launch morning.
- Rollback / abort trigger: if the failure threshold above ("What failure looks like" — FP rate > 30%) is hit by 2026-07-23, Jordan reverts the flag to the conservative-sensitivity 50% cohort and Marcus pauses the announcement email — owner Jordan Patel (eng).
