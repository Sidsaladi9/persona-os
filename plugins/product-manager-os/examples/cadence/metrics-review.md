**Definitions confirmed:** "Wk-2 retention" = % of teams that ran ≥1 check-in on days 8–14 after signup, cohorted by signup date (not a rolling snapshot). "Activation" = team ran ≥3 check-ins in first 7 days. "Member completion rate" = members who submitted / members prompted, among teams that are still active. Windows are Mon–Sun period totals.

## What moved and why
- 🔴 **Wk-2 retention −7pp (61% → 54%, target 64%)** — segmented by signup cohort, the entire drop sits in teams that onboarded **Jun 1–7** (this week's day-8–14 cohort): they fell to 49% vs. ~62% for every prior cohort. Cross-referencing with the notification logs, **31% of those teams never received their day-8 Slack check-in reminder** — a Slack rate-limit / token-refresh regression shipped with the **Jun 3 notifications refactor**. Teams that did get the reminder retained at 71% (in line with history). *Hypothesis: this is a delivery bug, not a demand problem — the habit reminder silently stopped firing for a third of the new cohort.*
- 🟡 **Wk-1 activation 72% (target 75%)** — flat vs. prior, slightly under plan but within normal variance; not the story this week. Worth noting the same Slack reminder path feeds first-week nudges, so a slice of the 3pp gap may share the same root cause — flagged to verify.
- 🟢 **Net MRR +$3.2k, seats +47** — healthy, on plan; no expansion or churn surprises this week.
- 🟢 **Digest opens & member completion flat-high** — managers and *already-active* members are engaged as normal. This confirms the problem is getting teams *back* in week 2, not the experience once they're in.

## Watch list (not red yet — monitor)
- **Wk-1 activation** 3pp under target two weeks running — could be downstream of the same notification regression; confirm before treating as a separate problem.
- **Member completion rate** −1pp — noise this week (within normal week-to-week variance, large denominator), but watch it doesn't compound if reminders stay flaky.

## Signal vs. noise
- The 🔴 is **signal**: large cohort (n=44 teams in the affected cohort), a clean segment split, and a named shipping event that lines up to the day. Not seasonality, not a small base.
- Member completion −1pp and activation +1pp are **noise** — small moves on large, stable denominators. Not dramatizing them.
