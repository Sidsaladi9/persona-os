# positioning — worked example (Cadence)

## TL;DR
- We position Cadence as the **async standup replacement** — the frame where "no more standup meetings, but managers still know what's happening" is the obvious win, instead of competing as a "Slack bot" where our digest reads as table stakes.
- Best fit is **engineering managers at 20–200 person tech companies running 2+ time zones**, where the standup meeting is most expensive and the manager's blocker-radar is weakest.
- Primary alternatives are **Geekbot** and **"just using Slack"**; our wedge is the **manager digest + Blocker Detector** that turns raw check-ins into a read-in-2-minutes signal, not a feed nobody reads.

REASONING (the 5 Dunford components, worked before the artifacts)

**1. Competitive alternatives — what a team actually does today**
- 🟢 Live standup on Zoom/in-person — the true status quo. 8 people × 15 min × 5 days = ~10 hrs/week of synchronous time, and it breaks the moment two people are in different time zones.
- 🟡 Geekbot — collects check-ins on a schedule and dumps the answers into a Slack channel. Removes the meeting; does **not** remove the reading.
- 🟡 "Just using Slack" — a recurring reminder and a thread. Free, zero new tools, and the manager scrolls a wall of updates (or doesn't).

**2. Unique attributes — what we have that the alternatives lack**
- An **AI digest** that compresses every team member's check-in into one manager-facing summary (themes, who's stuck, what moved) — not a raw transcript.
- **Blocker Detector** — flags at-risk work from the language of check-ins ("still waiting on…", "not sure how…", "blocked again") before it shows up in a missed deadline. [pre-GA]
- A check-in flow **built for async** (timezone-aware windows, per-person nudges) rather than a Slack bot bolted onto a channel.

**3. Value — what those attributes let the manager do**
- Kill the standup meeting **and still know the state of the team** in a 2-minute read — Geekbot and Slack give back the time but take away the manager's situational awareness; we give back the time and keep it.
- **See risk early.** The Blocker Detector surfaces the quiet "I'm stuck" that gets buried in a channel until it's a slipped sprint.
- Get check-ins that **actually happen across time zones** without anyone waking up for a call or babysitting a thread.

**4. Best-fit customer — who cares most**
Engineering managers / team leads at **20–200 person tech companies** running **distributed or multi-time-zone teams**, where (a) the live standup is most painful, (b) the manager has the least visibility, and (c) there's budget for tooling but no ops team to build it. They feel the digest value immediately because reading 8 raw Slack updates a day is already their problem.

**5. Frame of reference — the category that makes the value obvious**
"**Async standup replacement**." It sets the comparison against the *meeting* (10 expensive hours/week), where our digest + Blocker Detector are clearly net-new value. The "Slack check-in bot" frame buries us next to Geekbot, where the digest reads as a nice-to-have.

POSITIONING STATEMENT

For engineering managers at distributed 20–200 person tech companies who are losing hours to standup meetings that still don't surface what's stuck, Cadence is the async standup replacement: teams check in in writing on their own schedule, and managers get a 2-minute digest that flags at-risk work before it slips.

Unlike Geekbot or a Slack thread — which take the meeting away but leave the manager scrolling raw updates — we turn check-ins into a digest and actively flag blockers, so async doesn't cost you visibility.

MESSAGING PILLARS

2. **See the blocker before the slip.** — Proof: Blocker Detector flags at-risk work from check-in language; in a 6-team design-partner pilot it surfaced 9 of 11 blockers managers later confirmed were real. [flag: pre-GA] [flag: single pilot, n=6 teams]
3. **Built for async, so check-ins actually happen.** — Proof: timezone-aware windows + per-person nudges; multi-time-zone teams retain ~22pts better at week 4 than single-tz teams. [flag: self-reported cohort] Note: this is also where our own week-2 retention problem lives — strongest where teams are most distributed, weakest where Cadence looks most like a free Slack thread.

---

WHO IT'S NOT FOR

- **Co-located teams that like their standup.** If everyone's in one room at 9:30 and the meeting works, the async frame has no pain to relieve — they'll see us as Geekbot at a higher price.
- **Solo operators / 2–3 person teams.** No standup tax, no visibility gap; "just using Slack" is genuinely fine and we shouldn't pretend otherwise.
- **Buyers shopping purely on price.** We are above Geekbot ($4 vs $2.50/user/mo); if the digest and Blocker Detector aren't valued, we lose that comparison and should let it go rather than discount into it.
