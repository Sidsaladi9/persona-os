# Cadence Release Notes — v3.2 (week of June 14, 2026)

> **TL;DR**
> We shipped the Blocker Detector: every digest now flags at-risk work pulled straight from check-ins, so managers see what's stuck before standup would have. Rolling out to all Team and Enterprise plans, off by default for the first week. One thing to know: a few teams on the legacy Slack check-in format need to re-confirm their channel.

> **Headline:** The digest now surfaces at-risk work automatically — Blocker Detector reads every check-in and flags what's likely to slip, no manual triage.

---

## 1. User-facing changelog (benefit-led)

**Cadence v3.2 is here.** Your daily digest now does the reading for you — it scans every check-in and surfaces the work that looks stuck, so you walk into the day already knowing where to step in.

> ⚠️ **Action required:** Most teams: nothing to do — Blocker Detector turns on automatically next Monday (June 22). If your team still posts check-ins through the legacy Slack format (set up before March 2025), re-confirm your check-in channel in **Settings → Integrations** before June 22, or the digest will skip those check-ins.

### ✨ New
- **Blocker Detector in your digest** — Each digest now opens with a "Needs attention" section that flags work likely to slip, pulled from phrases like "still waiting on," "blocked by," and check-ins that went quiet two days running.
- **One-tap nudge from a flag** — See a flagged item, send the person a check-in reminder without leaving the digest.
- **"Why flagged" on every item** — Each flag shows the exact line from the check-in that triggered it, so you can tell a real blocker from a false alarm in a second.

### ⚡ Improved
- **Digests arrive faster** — Digest generation that used to land 8–10 minutes after the check-in window now lands in under 2 minutes for teams up to 40 people.
- **Quieter weekends** — Teams with weekend check-ins turned off no longer get an empty Saturday digest.

### 🐛 Fixed
- Fixed check-ins submitted in the last 30 seconds of the window occasionally not appearing in that day's digest.
- Fixed the mobile digest cutting off check-ins longer than ~200 words.
- Plus a batch of smaller fixes to timezone handling and email rendering in Outlook.

### 🔻 Deprecated / changing
- **Legacy Slack check-in format** — The pre-March-2025 Slack setup is being replaced by the current Integrations flow. It still works until **August 1, 2026**; re-connect your channel in **Settings → Integrations** any time before then.

## 2. Internal release log (what / why / impact)

**Release:** v3.2 · **Date:** 2026-06-17 · **Reach:** all Team + Enterprise (Blocker Detector flag-gated, default ON June 22) · **Owner:** Priya Nair (PM)

| Change | Type | Why (problem / bet) | Impact (surfaces · teams · metrics) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blocker Detector — at-risk work flagged in digest | New | Bet that managers churn because the digest is a passive recap, not an action prompt. Surfacing risk gives the digest a reason to open daily. | Surfaces: digest (web + email + mobile). Watch: digest open rate, flag click-through, false-flag report rate. Support: expect "why was this flagged?" — point to the "Why flagged" line. |
| One-tap nudge from a flagged item | New | Reduce the manager's effort from "notice → switch to Slack → DM" to one action. | Surfaces: digest. Teams: ties into existing reminder pipeline. Watch: nudge-send rate per flag. |
| Detection model + "Why flagged" rationale | New | Trust gate — managers won't act on a black-box flag. Showing the source line keeps flags auditable. | Eng/on-call: new inference call in digest generation path; adds ~1.5s/digest. Watch p95 digest latency. |
| Digest generation latency cut | Improved | 8–10 min lag meant some managers checked Slack before the digest landed, undercutting the product. | Surfaces: all digests. Re-architected the batch job; watch error rate on the new path for one week. |
| Empty weekend digest suppressed | Fixed | Teams with weekends off still got a blank digest — minor but a recurring "is this broken?" ticket. | Support: closes a known low-volume ticket cluster. |
| Last-30s check-in drop | Fixed | Race condition: check-ins submitted at the window edge missed the digest cutoff. | Affected ~check-ins at the boundary; no comms needed, low incidence. |
| Mobile long-check-in truncation | Fixed | Mobile capped display at ~200 words, hiding detail managers needed. | Surfaces: mobile digest only. |
| Legacy Slack check-in format | Deprecated | Two ingestion paths doubled maintenance and caused the flag model to miss legacy-format check-ins. | Replacement: current Integrations flow. Still works until **Aug 1, 2026**. Migration: re-connect channel. ~6% of teams still on legacy — see support note. |
| Timezone + Outlook email rendering | Infra/Internal | Cleanup bundled with the digest rewrite. | No user impact beyond the fixes above; noted for on-call context. |

> **Breaking vs. Deprecated:** **Breaking** = user must migrate now or it breaks on ship. **Deprecated** = still works until a named sunset date with a named replacement. The legacy Slack format is **Deprecated** (works until Aug 1), not Breaking — but the ~6% of teams on it must re-connect before **June 22** to get Blocker Detector coverage, so treat that subset as time-sensitive.

**Watch after ship:** Week-2 retention for teams that get their first flag in week 1 (the core bet) · digest open rate · false-flag report rate (kill switch if >15%) · p95 digest latency.
**Known gaps / follow-ups:** Blocker Detector is read-only this release — no auto-assignment or Jira/Linear write-back yet. Detection is English-only; non-English check-ins are not flagged. Both are v3.3 candidates.
**Support / sales notes:** Predictable inbound — *"How does the flag decide what's at risk?"* Canned answer: it reads check-in text and cadence (blocker language + check-ins that went quiet), shows the exact source line, and never auto-escalates — the manager decides. For legacy-Slack teams asking why they see no flags: have them re-connect their channel in Settings → Integrations.
