# Market Sizing: Async standup + AI manager digest for engineering teams in 20–200-person tech companies (global, English-first)
Prepared: 2026-06-17 · Author: PM, Cadence · Data mode: provided only (figures below are stated assumptions, not live-pulled — see "What would change this number")

**TL;DR**
- We can defensibly claim a ~$340M SAM and a ~$22M obtainable ARR over 4 years — roughly 7× our current ~$3M, a real but disciplined target.
- Bottom-up and top-down land within ~1.6× on an independent basis (our ARR ~$3M vs. a ~$45–60M tracked-competitor revenue pool), so the convergence is credible, not circular.
- The whole number hinges on one input: paid seats per team. We model 8; at 4 the SAM halves. We should instrument this before we quote SAM externally.

## Market definition
> Async written check-ins ("check-in") plus an AI manager "digest" — priced per seat — for engineering and product teams inside 20–200-employee tech companies, sold to engineering managers and team leads, global English-first, over a 4-year horizon.

| **TAM** (total demand) | ~$1.9B | ~$2.0B | **~$1.9B** |

One-line takeaway: A ~$22M obtainable ARR in 4 years (≈7× today's ~$3M) is worth pursuing — it's defensible against the tracked-competitor revenue pool and within sales capacity — provided week-2 retention is fixed first, because the SOM assumes teams actually stick.

## Bottom-up build

TAM = `[all dev/PM teams] × [seats/team] × [annual price]` with NO penetration applied. Penetration below = **share of teams that fit / could buy**, not win-share (win-share enters only at SOM).

## Reconciliation
- Top-down SAM: ~$330M · Bottom-up SAM: ~$345M · Gap: ~1.05× (within 2×).
- Independent basis? **Partially independent.** Top-down anchors on a seat-spend total (seats × blended ASP); bottom-up uses our own seats/team × our $48 ACV. The price anchor is shared, so the layers are not fully independent — but the seat-count bases differ (industry seat census vs. our cohort build-up). For the SOM-level credibility check we use a genuinely independent anchor: the tracked-competitor revenue pool (Geekbot + Standuply + adjacent async tools, est. ~$45–60M combined ARR) vs. our ~$3M today.
- Within 2× → converge on SAM **$330M–$345M**.
- Converged SAM: **~$340M** (range $330M–$345M).

## SOM (obtainable)
- **Unit:** ARR (per-seat subscription business).
- SAM ~$340M × ~6.5% target win-share over 4 yrs = **~$22M ARR**.
- Justification: We're at ~$3M / ~4,000 teams today (≈1.3% of the ~315K fitting teams by count). Reaching ~6.5% of SAM by ARR means roughly tripling team count and holding seats/team — credible only if week-2 retention improves. Competitive density is moderate (Geekbot is the leader; "just use Slack" is the real default). Win-share capped single-digit because incumbents + free-Slack inertia hold most of SAM.

## Sanity checks
- [x] Implied market share vs. leader (Geekbot est. ~$20–30M ARR): a $22M Cadence ARR would put us at rough parity with the leader in year 4, not ahead of it — **pass** (aggressive but not fantasy).
- [x] Implied CAGR — current ARR ~$3M → SOM ~$22M over 4 yrs requires ~65%/yr: **pass** — high but within Series-A→B SaaS ramp bounds for a category with a clear default-replacement wedge; comparable async tools have sustained 50–80% early.
- [x] SOM vs. realistic sales capacity — PLG-led: ~$22M ÷ ~$384 ACV/team (8 seats × $48) ≈ 57K paying teams. We're at ~4K. Net-add ~13K teams/yr via self-serve is plausible **only with** funnel + activation work — **pass with caveat** (gated on retention).
- [x] Penetration vs. comparable adoption curve — 6.5% of fitting teams in 4 yrs tracks observed async-tool adoption in dev orgs — **pass**.
- [x] Scope discipline — every layer traces to "20–200-person tech co's, dev/PM teams, per-seat, English-first" — **yes**.

## Load-bearing assumption
- **Paid seats per team = 8.** It multiplies through TAM, SAM, and SOM linearly.
- If it's half (4 seats): SAM ≈ $170M, SOM ≈ $11M. If double (16, unrealistic given ~10-seat teams): SAM ≈ $690M, SOM ≈ $44M.
- Confidence: med · How to firm it up: pull actual paid-seats-per-team distribution from billing across the full ~4,000-team base (we quoted cohort median, not the fleet-wide mean — likely lower).

## What would change this number
- A billing-pull on **real paid seats/team across all 4,000 teams** (replaces the cohort-median estimate — the single biggest swing).
- A **published count of 20–200-employee tech companies in our English-first markets** (firms up the 9% penetration term, currently an estimate).
- A confirmed **competitor ARR figure** (Geekbot/Standuply) to harden the independent top-down anchor and the year-4 parity claim.
