# okrs — worked example (Cadence)

> Generated by the Product Manager OS `okrs` skill. Demo company: Cadence.

---

**TL;DR**
- One Objective this quarter (Q3 2026): make a team's second week as sticky as its first, with the Blocker Detector as the lever.
- Three KRs — two aspirational (week-2 retention, Blocker Detector adoption), one committed guardrail (digest open rate must not regress).
- Status: 🟡 at risk. Both growth KRs depend on the Blocker Detector reaching GA by Aug 8; we've written a re-baselining rule so a slip doesn't auto-score the quarter to zero.

## Inputs we gathered

- **Higher-level goal this ladders to:** Company H2 goal — "Get teams past the cold-start cliff so they renew." Net revenue retention target 92% → 100% by year-end; week-2 check-in retention is the leading indicator the company GM is tracking.
- **Metric we can move + owner:** Week-2 team retention (% of teams that posted a check-in on ≥3 days in their second week, given they were active in week 1). Owned by Priya (PM), instrumented in Amplitude cohort `team_week2_active`.
- **Timeframe:** Q3 2026 (Jul 1 – Sep 30).
- **Baselines (pulled Jun 16, 2026):**
  - Week-2 team retention: **41%** (Amplitude, trailing 8-week cohort avg; ~210 teams/week reach week 2).
  - Manager digest open rate: **63%** (Amplitude `digest_opened` / `digest_sent`, 30-day).
  - Blocker Detector adoption: **0%** — not GA yet. Closed beta with 18 teams; GA targeted **Aug 8**.
- **Team capacity note:** 1 PM, 3 engineers (Marco on PTO 3 days this sprint), 1 designer, shared analyst (Dana, ~1 day/week).

## OKR draft

```
OBJECTIVE: Make a team's second week as sticky as its first — turn the cold-start cliff into a habit.
  Ladders up to: Company H2 — get teams past the cold-start cliff so they renew (NRR 92% → 100%).
  Timeframe: Q3 2026 (Jul 1 – Sep 30)

KEY RESULTS (outcomes — tag each [committed] or [aspirational]):
  KR1 — Week-2 team retention | baseline 41% → target 55% | source: Amplitude cohort team_week2_active | [aspirational]
  KR2 — Teams with ≥1 Blocker Detector flag acted on in week 2 (acted = reply, reassign, or resolve) | baseline 0% → target 35% of week-2 teams | source: Amplitude event blocker_flag_actioned | [aspirational]
        GATING DATE: Blocker Detector GA = Aug 8. If GA slips past Aug 22, re-baseline target to the weeks actually remaining (rule below) rather than scoring 0.0.
  KR_g — Manager digest open rate | hold ≥ 60% (target 63%) | source: Amplitude digest_opened/digest_sent | [committed]

INITIATIVES (the work that drives the KRs — NOT key results):
  - Ship Blocker Detector to GA (flags at-risk work from check-ins) → drives KR1, KR2
  - Week-2 re-engagement nudge: in-product + email prompt to teams that skip day 8–10 → drives KR1
  - Onboarding redesign: lighter day-1 setup, default check-in cadence pre-filled → drives KR1
  - Blocker Detector "first flag" moment in the digest (surface the flag where managers already look) → drives KR2, protects KR_g
  - Digest relevance pass: suppress empty-state digests that trained managers to ignore them → protects KR_g

HOW WE'LL SCORE & CHECK IN:
  Cadence: weekly (Mon, 15 min), owner: Priya (PM)
  Scoring: 0.0–1.0 per KR (1.0 = target, ~0.7 = strong progress)
    KR1: aspirational — 0.7 (≈51%) is a win
    KR2: aspirational — 0.7 (≈25%) is a win; re-baselined if GA slips
    KR_g: committed — must hold ≥60%; below floor = miss regardless of KR1/KR2

RISKS / SANDBAGGING CHECK:
  - Too soft? KR_g at "hold ≥60%" is close to baseline (63%) — but it's a deliberate guardrail, not a growth bet. Acceptable.
  - Too hard? KR1 (41% → 55%, +14pts in one quarter) is the stretch. ~0.7-likely if the nudge + onboarding redesign land early; full 55% needs the Blocker Detector pulling weight too.
  - Launch-coupled: KR2 (and part of KR1) can't move until Blocker Detector GA on Aug 8 — only ~7 weeks of signal. Both growth KRs lean on one dependency, which is the biggest risk in this set (see below).
  - Baselines we're unsure of: KR2 baseline is 0% by definition (not GA); the 35% target is anchored on the 18-team beta where ~40% acted on a flag — small sample, treat as a hypothesis.
```

| Launch-coupling concentration | 🔴 → mitigated | Both growth KRs depend on the Aug 8 GA. Mitigation: KR1 also moves via the nudge + onboarding work (not GA-dependent), so a slip dents but doesn't zero the quarter. Re-baselining rule below. |

## Re-baselining rule (agreed up front)

If Blocker Detector GA slips past **Aug 22**, we do not score KR2 at 0.0. Instead, at GA we re-cut the target to the weeks remaining: target = 35% × (weeks of post-GA signal left ÷ 7), rounded to the nearest 5%. KR1's target holds — its non-GA initiatives (nudge, onboarding) carry it — but we note the Blocker Detector contribution as forfeited and review whether 55% is still reachable at the Sep 1 check-in.
