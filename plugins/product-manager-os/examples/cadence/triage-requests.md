## Verdicts in detail

### ▶ BUILD — Teams quietly stop checking in after week 1
- **Job to be done:** A team adopts Cadence, checks in for a week, then drops off — reminders feel naggy and badly timed, so the habit never sets.
- **Demand:** 4 requests · 4 distinct teams, and the $90K Northwind account named this directly (#11) alongside its renewal.
- **Why now:** This is priority (1) for the quarter. Smarter reminder timing (timezone-aware, adaptive cadence) is the most-named lever and directly attacks week-2 retention.
- **Next step:** Route to spec — "adaptive check-in reminders" (timezone-aware + escalating nudge logic). Roadmap slot: **Now**.
- **Open question:** Is the drop-off driven by reminder timing, or by the check-in feeling low-value once the novelty fades? Pull the week-1→week-2 funnel with the data analyst before we scope.

### ▶ BUILD — Surface at-risk work before standup
- **Job to be done:** Managers read the digest but can't see what's about to slip until it already has — they want the risk flagged, not just a list of updates.
- **Demand:** 2 requests · 2 distinct teams · clean fit with the Blocker Detector bet.
- **Why now:** This is priority (2) — the Blocker Detector launch. These two asks are unprompted validation of the exact problem statement.
- **Next step:** Fold into the Blocker Detector spec as a primary use case; use #5 and #6 as design partners. Roadmap slot: **Now / Next**.
- **Open question:** Do managers want a passive flag in the digest, or an active alert outside it? Confirm with the designer before we commit the surface.

### ⏸ WATCH — Enterprise SSO / SCIM gating IT review
- **Demand:** 3 requests · ~$126K touched across Northwind ($90K renewal), Brightside ($22K new), Orbit Health ($14K renewal).
- **Why not now:** Genuinely off-theme. SSO/SCIM is admin/IT/security tooling — explicitly not what we're building this quarter, and it's an **L** that would pull an engineer off retention during a sprint already short one person (PTO). The $90K is real, but raw revenue pressure does not turn an off-strategy ask into a Build; the dollar risk goes to whoever owns the deal, not the roadmap.
- **Re-trigger threshold:** Revisit if SSO blocks **>$200K in combined ARR** or **>3 enterprise renewals** in a quarter — at that point it's a strategy input, not a one-off.
- **Deal-desk routing (this is where the $90K goes):** For Northwind, do not absorb SSO into the roadmap. Options for the deal owner (Priya + exec): (a) a written commitment that SSO lands next quarter to clear the security review, (b) a short-term Okta-via-SCIM-free workaround / contractual security addendum, or (c) a renewal discount in exchange for staying on current auth. Same playbook for Orbit's IT review.

### ✕ DECLINE — Microsoft Teams surface (beyond Slack)
- **Demand:** 1 request · 1 team / $8K.
- **Why we're saying no:** Off-strategy (integrations breadth) and an **L** build for a single deal. Serves <0.1% of the base today.
- **Alternative offered:** Email + web check-in already work without Slack; tracked but not planned. Re-weigh if Teams demand clusters across multiple deals.

### ✕ DECLINE — Jira / ticket sync into check-in
- **Demand:** 1 distinct team (4 tickets from one admin — volume is one loud voice, not breadth).
- **Why we're saying no:** Off-theme (integrations breadth), and 4 tickets from a single account is not 4 units of demand. No reach beyond Tandem.
- **Alternative offered:** Zapier/webhook can post Jira status into a check-in today; we'll share the recipe. Logged so we'd notice if it spreads.

### ✕ DECLINE — Custom-branded / white-label digest
- **Demand:** 1 request · 1 team / $11K.
- **Why we're saying no:** Per-team customization — explicitly off-strategy this quarter, niche, and an **M** with no retention or Blocker-Detector value.
- **Alternative offered:** None planned; logged. Point Vector to the existing digest, which already carries their workspace name.
