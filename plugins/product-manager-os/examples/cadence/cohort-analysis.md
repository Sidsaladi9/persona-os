# Cohort Retention Analysis — Cadence (new teams, all plans)

**Date:** 2026-06-17 · **Analyst:** Priya Nair (PM)
**Definition:** Cohort = teams by signup week. Retained = ≥1 member posts a check-in that week (a value moment, not a login). Grain = weekly — matches the product's expected weekday-daily / minimum weekly rhythm.
**P0 denominator:** P0 = activated teams only (a team where ≥2 members posted a check-in in week 0). This choice changes every downstream %.
**Signup→P0 activation rate:** 71% (412 signups → 292 activated). *(An activated-P0 table conceals tire-kicker churn: ~29% of teams that sign up never get two people to post once. That pre-activation leak is real and not visible in the table below — flagged here so we don't read 71% as 100%.)*
**Window:** signup weeks of Apr 6 – May 25 2026 (8 cohorts) · **Assumptions:** "retained" requires a posted check-in, not a digest open; a team counts active in a period if it posts in ≥1 of that week's working days.

**Benchmark band:** ~70–80% P1 / ~40–45% floor is typical for B2B SaaS team-collaboration tools with a weekly habit loop. Read every number against this band, not against zero. Cadence's P1 is on-band; the P1→P2 fall is the off-band problem.

## What the curve says

- **The cliff (P1→P2): 🔴 ~34-point drop (79% → 45%)** — the largest adjacent fall by far. Scanning every pair: P0→P1 = −21pts, **P1→P2 = −34pts**, P2→P3 = −4pts, P3→P4 = −3pts, P4→P5 = 0pts. The cliff is week 2, not day 1. A late cliff like this points at a **habit / value-depth problem**, not onboarding — teams get set up and check in for week 1, then the routine fails to stick.
- **Curve shape:** Flattens hard after P2. From P2 onward it's nearly flat (45 → 41 → 38 → 38), so this is **not** a leaky bucket — there's a real retained core. The damage is concentrated in the single P1→P2 step.
- **Retention floor:** ~38% — the durable core that keeps a weekly check-in habit. On-band for the category, but we're leaving ~34 points on the floor at the week-2 step. Floor is enough to build on; the cliff is what's capping growth.
- **Cohort-over-cohort (read down a column):** P1 is creeping up (79% → 84% across Apr 6 → May 18) 🟢 — plausibly tied to the revised setup checklist shipped wk of May 4. But P2 is flat (47% → 44% → 45% → 48% → 46% → 49%) 🟡 — nothing we've shipped has moved the week-2 step. We're improving the part that wasn't broken.
- **Inflection point:** Most teams lost at **P2** relative to value delivered. One intervention at the week-2 transition has the most leverage of anywhere in the curve.

The blended ~38% floor hides two very different teams: sales-assisted teams that saw a human set up the check-in cadence retain nearly 2x the self-serve teams. The week-2 cliff is overwhelmingly a self-serve problem — those teams hit week 2 without anyone having modeled what a sustained check-in habit looks like.

## Hypothesis → action

- **Hypothesis:** Self-serve teams drop at **P2** because week-1 novelty fades and nothing re-anchors the habit. The manager gets the week-1 digest, sees value once, then check-in participation decays before the routine is automatic. There's no nudge at the exact moment the habit is most fragile (start of week 2).
- **Evidence:** P1→P2 is the only material fall (−34pts) and the curve is flat on either side; the gap is concentrated in self-serve (77%→31% by P3 vs. 84%→56% sales-assisted); P2 has not moved cohort-over-cohort despite the May setup-checklist ship, confirming onboarding isn't the lever.
- **Action:** Ship a **start-of-week-2 re-anchor** for self-serve teams: a Monday-of-week-2 digest to the manager that names who's slipping, plus an in-Slack nudge to members who checked in during week 1 but haven't yet in week 2. (This is also the natural first proving ground for the Blocker Detector — surface a flagged at-risk item in that week-2 digest to give the manager a concrete reason to keep the habit.)
- **Success metric:** P2 retention for self-serve cohorts rises from **~45% → 58%** (closing roughly half the gap to sales-assisted) for cohorts signing up after the ship date.
- **Re-check:** Re-pull this table 4 weeks after ship. Watch the **P2 column down the self-serve cohorts** specifically — that's the only cell the intervention should move first. Do not declare a win off the two youngest cohorts (small N, incomplete periods).
