## 4. Blameless postmortem (for PM / leadership)

**Incident:** Morning manager digest not sending · **Severity:** SEV2 · **Duration:** 2026-07-19 08:00–10:18 PT (2h 18m) · **Author:** Marcus Reyes (PM) · **Date:** 2026-07-19

**TL;DR:** A Blocker Detector config change shipped the night before added enough per-check-in processing that the 8:00am digest job timed out for the half of Team-plan teams with flags enabled — ~2,100 teams missed their morning digest email. No check-in data was lost or exposed; in-app access was never affected. The digest was fixed and re-sent by 10:18am. Resolved.

**Customer impact**
- **Who:** ~2,100 of ~4,000 Team-plan teams (the Blocker Detector 50% rollout cohort), including 11 design-partner teams in the beta.
- **What they experienced:** No 8:00am digest email; the day's at-a-glance "what's stuck" view was missing for ~2.3 hours. Check-in data was always intact and viewable in-app.
- **Business impact:** No data loss, no SLA breach (Team plan has no delivery SLA). Soft cost: the digest is our core retention surface, and this hit during the Blocker Detector beta when design-partner trust matters most. 14 support conversations; 3 design-partner leads emailed directly.

**Timeline** (all PT)
| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| 07-18 21:40 | Blocker Detector sensitivity config change merged and deployed |
| 07-19 08:00 | Digest job starts; flag-scoring step runs long for the enabled cohort |
| 08:08 | Job exceeds time budget and times out for ~2,100 teams; digests not sent |
| 08:12 | Digest-success-rate alert fires (<60%); on-call paged |
| 08:24 | First customer-facing status update posted |
| 09:00 | Cause confirmed: config-added latency × digest volume = timeout |
| 09:40 | Fix deployed (config reverted + job time budget raised); re-send begins |
| 10:18 | All affected digests re-sent and confirmed delivered — resolved |

**Root cause (plain language):** A new Blocker Detector sensitivity setting added a per-check-in scoring step to the morning digest job. At full digest volume, that extra work pushed the job past its time limit and it stopped before sending to the flag-enabled half of teams. The change had been validated for *accuracy* but not for its *runtime cost at scale*, and the digest job had no headroom alert to catch the creep. (See the engineering RCA for the job-timeout and query-level detail.)

**What went well / what hurt**
- ✅ Alert fired 12 minutes after the failure window — detection worked, no customer had to tell us first.
- ✅ Check-in data was never at risk; the failure was isolated to email delivery, so a clean re-send fully recovered it.
- ⚠️ The config change shipped with no load/runtime check against digest volume — accuracy was tested, cost wasn't.
- ⚠️ First customer update went out 24 minutes after detection; for a core-surface SEV2 we want it inside 15.

**Prevention — action items**
| Action | Type | Owner | Due |
|--------|------|-------|-----|
| Add a digest-job runtime budget alert that warns at 70% of the timeout, before it fails | Detect | Jordan Patel | 2026-07-25 |
| Require a load/runtime check (not just accuracy) in the pre-merge checklist for any change touching the digest job | Prevent | Marcus Reyes | 2026-07-24 |
| Make the digest job degrade gracefully — send the base digest and append flags when ready, instead of failing whole | Mitigate | Priya Chandra | 2026-08-01 |
| Pre-write status-page + in-app templates for digest-delivery incidents so first comms lands in <15 min | Mitigate | Marcus Reyes | 2026-07-23 |

### Why this is the shape it is
- **Severity set the cadence, and the cadence built the trust.** SEV2 → 60-minute updates, each one stamped with the next update time. Even the 09:05 "we've identified it" carried "next update by 10:00am" — customers waited with a deadline instead of wondering if anyone was looking.
- **The internal note and the customer note are deliberately different.** Internal got the unconfirmed theory, the scope %, the cohort name, and a verbatim support holding line. Customers got impact + reassurance (data is safe) + next update. The theory never went public until it was confirmed — and even then only in plain language in the postmortem.
- **No invented ETA, ever.** The first message promised a *next update time*, not a fix time. A re-send time was only stated once eng confirmed the data was retained and re-sendable.
- **The postmortem is blameless and aimed at leadership.** The cause is "a config change added runtime cost that wasn't load-tested," not "Jordan's deploy broke the digest." Action items are system/process fixes (alerting, a checklist gate, graceful degradation, comms templates), each with an owner and a date — and it points to the eng RCA rather than reproducing it.
