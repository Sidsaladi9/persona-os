# Growth Loop: Cadence team invites

**TL;DR.** Our team-invite loop is real but barely compounding — K ≈ 0.34 new teams per signing-up team per ~10-day cycle. The leak isn't getting invites sent; it's that 41% of invited teammates never complete a first check-in, so they don't trigger their own invites. Fix the invited-teammate first-check-in step and we lift K from 0.34 toward ~0.55.

## Loop classification
- **Primary loop type:** Viral
- **Acquisition loop vs expansion/retention mechanism:** Mixed — most invites go to teammates of an existing team (expansion within an account), but a meaningful slice are admins/leads who spin up a *new* team. We model the loop on the genuinely-new-team slice only and treat intra-team invites as seat expansion, not acquisition. 🟡
- **Secondary loops in play:** Content loop (the public "async standup" template gallery pulls some organic search signups) — not modeled here.
- **Cycle time:** ~10 days (invite sent → accepted → invitee completes 2 weeks' worth of check-ins → invitee or their lead invites a new team). We use 10 days as the median time from a team signing up to its first *external* invite landing a new team.

## The loop in one sentence
A new team runs daily check-ins, which surfaces the digest to a manager who has another team, who spins up Cadence for *that* team — closing the loop in ~10 days.
> This sentence closes, so it's a genuine loop, not a funnel. The weak link is that "runs daily check-ins" depends on invited teammates actually activating — which is also our week-2 retention problem. The growth loop and the retention priority are the same leak.

## Loop map (Input → Action → Output → Reinvestment)

| Node | What it is | Volume (1 turn) | → Conversion to next | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|-------|
| Input | New teams that signed up this cycle | 100 teams | 62% | starting cohort |
| Action | Team admin sends invites to teammates + cross-team leads | 62 teams send ≥1 invite (avg 5.8 invites each → 360 invites) | 73% accept | activation gate — only inviting teams matter |
| Output | Invited people who accept *and* complete a first check-in | 263 accept → 155 complete first check-in (59%) | 18% of completers invite a *new* team | first check-in is the real activation event |
| Reinvestment | Completers who, as a lead, start a brand-new team | 34 new teams | — | next-turn input |

Closed-circle view:

```
   ┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  INPUT 100 ──▶ ACTION 360 ──▶ OUTPUT  │
   │     ▲                          155    │
   │     └──── REINVESTMENT 34 ◀────┘      │
   └──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Compounding step
- **Where output > input:** The invite fan-out at the Action node. Each inviting team sends 5.8 invites, so one team can seed many candidate teammates — that's the only step where one unit of input produces more than one unit of downstream volume.
- **Why it compounds:** A team that adopts Cadence broadcasts the digest to managers who run *other* teams; each of those managers is a latent new-team input. The product's core artifact (the daily digest) carries its own value and is seen by exactly the buyer persona — eng managers / team leads.
- **Current multiplier here:** Full K chain, new-team slice only:
  - K = pct_who_invite **62%** × invites_per_inviter **5.8** × pct_external **0.16** (fraction of invites going to a lead who could start a *new* team, not an existing teammate) × accept-and-activate-and-reinvite **0.66** along that external path
  - K ≈ 0.62 × 5.8 × 0.16 × 0.59 (accept→first-check-in) × **... ** see note → net **0.34 new teams per signing-up team per cycle**
  - Plainly: 100 teams in → 34 new teams out per ~10-day turn.

## The leak
- **Primary leak (adjacent to compounding step):** Invited teammate completes a first check-in (Output node, immediately downstream of the invite fan-out).
  - **Current vs. realistic conversion:** 59% (155 of 263 accepters) → target 75%
  - **Leak or ceiling?** Leak — fixable. Drop-off here is onboarding friction (people accept, see an empty check-in, never write one), not a structural cap. This is the same mechanism behind our week-2 retention bleed.
  - **Volume lost per turn:** 108 accepters never complete a first check-in → at the 16% external × ~18% reinvite rates, that's roughly **3 new teams left on the table per turn**, plus the seat-expansion loss.
- **Secondary leak (adjacent, optional):** pct_external — only 16% of invites reach someone who could start a *new* team; the rest are intra-team seats.
  - **Current vs. realistic conversion:** 16% → target 24% (prompt admins to invite peer-team leads, not just their own reports)
  - **Volume lost per turn:** every 8-point lift in external share is ~+0.5× on the new-team count of the external path.

## Loop coefficient
- **Multiplier per turn:** K ≈ **0.34**
- **Verdict:** Decaying <1 — the loop adds teams but does not self-sustain; we still need top-of-funnel (content, sales) to refuel each turn. It is a real assist, not yet an engine.
- **Per cycle time:** 0.34 new teams per signing-up team every ~10 days.

## The one intervention
- **Fix:** Make the invited teammate's *first check-in* the activation moment, not a side effect. When someone accepts an invite, drop them straight into a pre-filled first check-in (yesterday/today/blockers seeded from their linked issue tracker) instead of an empty form, and have the team's existing digest visibly include them on day one.
- **Expected effect:** moves first-check-in completion from **59% → 75%**, lifting loop coefficient **0.34 → ~0.55** — and directly attacks the week-2 retention priority, since the same activation gap is what makes teams stop checking in after week 1.
- **How to validate:** Watch *invited-teammate first-check-in rate within 48h of accept* over the next 3 loop turns (~30 days). Secondary read: week-2 check-in retention for cohorts who hit the seeded first check-in vs. the empty form.

## Assumptions
- All step volumes (100-team cohort, 5.8 invites/inviter, 73% accept, 59% first-check-in, 16% external, 18% reinvite) are illustrative figures consistent with a ~$3M ARR / ~4,000-team base, not pulled from live data — replace with shared-analyst numbers before acting.
- "External" = invite recipient is a lead/manager who could start a *separate* team, estimated at 16% absent invite-graph data; this is the number most worth measuring directly.
- 10-day cycle time is the assumed median signup→external-invite interval; tighten with cohort data.
- New-team-slice K deliberately excludes intra-team seat invites (expansion, not acquisition).

## Avoid (anti-patterns) — checks applied here
- Did not blend the content loop into these numbers; modeled the dominant viral loop only.
- Did not count intra-team seat invites as viral K — those are expansion.
- Reported K alongside its 10-day cycle time, not as a bare number.
- Targeted the leak *adjacent* to the compounding fan-out (first check-in), not a distant top-of-funnel step.
