## RACI check

_This initiative bundles two real decisions — they get separate A's._

**Decision: go/no-go to ship this quarter**
- **Accountable (exactly one):** Dana (VP Product) — confirmed; owns the Q3 roadmap.
- **Responsible:** Priya (PM), Sofia (Design), the 1.5 allocated engineers.
- **Consulted (before the decision):** Elena (CEO), Marcus (Eng Dir), Tomás (Data).
- **Informed (after):** Will (CS), Raj (Finance).

**Decision: allocation of 1.5 of 3 engineers**
- **Accountable (exactly one):** Marcus (Eng Dir) — confirmed; owns eng capacity.
- **Responsible:** Marcus + the named engineers.
- **Consulted (before the decision):** Dana (VP Product), Priya (PM).
- **Informed (after):** Elena (CEO), Raj (Finance).
- ⚠️ The two A's are intentionally split. If they get collapsed into one "Blocker Detector decision," the real risk — Marcus quietly under-resourcing it while saying yes to the ship — gets hidden. Keep them separate.

## Risks & watch-items

- **Skeptic with power:** Marcus controls the engineers. If he stays skeptical, we get a "yes" on shipping but no people to ship it — the worst outcome. Neutralize by scoping v1 small and protecting retention work; he's the swing voter here.
- **Split ownership is load-bearing, not a problem:** the go/no-go and eng-allocation A's must stay separate. The failure mode is Dana saying go while Marcus under-allocates. Surface both A's explicitly in the review.
- **Unverified supporter:** we're assuming Elena (CEO) backs this based on a hallway comment, not a direct statement — ❓. The 3-line pre-read doubles as a quiet confirmation check.
- **Coalition:** Dana + Will + Sofia are aligned on "retention via better digests" and will pull in our favor. The counter-pull is Marcus + (passively) Raj on "protect current capacity." Winning Marcus collapses the opposition.
- **Naming note:** keep any internal codename out of the early-access materials Will shares with teams — customer-facing surface uses "Blocker Detector" only.
